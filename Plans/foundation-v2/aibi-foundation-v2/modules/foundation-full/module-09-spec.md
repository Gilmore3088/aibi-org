# Module 9: Your AI Work Profile

**Pillar:** Understanding · **Duration:** 20 min · **Track:** Foundation Full
**Activity types used:** 5 (build & test — task fit calculator), 8 (real-world capture)
**Daily-use outcomes:** AI Work Profile with top-3 candidates and personal boundaries

---

## Why this module exists

The course has, by this point, given the learner: data discipline, threat awareness, member-conversation skill, prompt mastery, and regulatory routing. Module 9 turns the lens inward and asks: **for *your* job, where does AI actually fit?**

The activity is a guided self-inventory that produces the AI Work Profile — the bridge to Module 10 (Projects), Module 16 (Role-Based Use Cases), and Module 17 (Personal Prompt Library).

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the learner will:

1. Inventory their recurring tasks across daily, weekly, and monthly cadences
2. Classify each task by AI fit and data tier
3. Identify their top 3 high-fit, safe-tier candidates for AI assistance
4. Articulate at least 5 personal boundaries — tasks they will *not* use AI for

---

## Activities

### Activity 9.1 — Inventory your week (8 min · type 8)

**Setup (1 min):** "List your recurring tasks. The platform will help you classify each."

**The activity:**

The platform presents an inventory builder with three sections (daily / weekly / monthly).

For each task entered, the platform asks (one screen per task):

1. **Task name and approximate time per occurrence**
2. **AI fit** — adaptive question: "Is this primarily about generating language, looking up facts, making decisions, or running production?"
 - Language → likely high-fit
 - Lookup → likely partial-fit (verify with grounded Q&A)
 - Decision → AI assists, never decides
 - Production → no fit
3. **Data tier** — the platform shows examples and asks the learner to pick the correct tier
4. **Frequency** — how often per the cadence (e.g., 15× per day, 3× per week)

The platform stops when the learner has entered at least 8 tasks across cadences.

**Capture:** task inventory with classifications → Section 1–2 of the AI Work Profile.

### Activity 9.2 — Find your top 3 (5 min · type 5)

**Setup (1 min):** "The platform will rank your tasks by AI value. You confirm the top 3."

**The activity:**
1. Platform applies a scoring rubric to the learner's inventory:
 - High-fit + safe-tier (Public/Internal) + high frequency → high score
 - Partial-fit + safe-tier + medium frequency → medium score
 - High-fit but Confidential/Restricted → flagged "approval needed"
 - Decision tasks → flagged "AI assists only"
2. Platform displays a ranked list. Learner picks their top 3.
3. For each top-3, learner fills in: why this is high-fit, expected risk profile, time-to-value estimate.

**Capture:** top-3 candidates → Section 3 of the AI Work Profile.

### Activity 9.3 — Set your boundaries (5 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "Equally important: name what AI will *not* do for you. The platform will help."

**The activity:**

Platform shows the standard boundary categories with examples and asks the learner to pick or write at least 5:

- Decisions (which decisions you alone make)
- Sensitive communications (condolence, hardship, internal investigations)
- Regulator/examiner correspondence
- Anything with member NPI on a public AI tool (always)
- Tasks where your voice is the value (relationships, sensitive member calls)
- Current/specific facts (rates, regulations, prices) without verification
- Anything not worth the data-tier evaluation overhead

Platform sends the picks + any free-form additions to a default model with the prompt: *"Format these into a clear, calm 'I will not use AI for...' list under 200 words. Match the voice of a community-bank professional. Don't add items I didn't mention."*

Learner reviews, edits, signs.

**Capture:** Section 4 of the AI Work Profile.

### Activity 9.4 — Manager alignment prompt (1 min · reading)

> *"Your manager should see this Profile. The platform has emailed you a copy and a request you forward to your manager for review. The Profile is yours; the visibility is good for the team."*

---

## Daily-use outcomes

1. A complete **AI Work Profile** with task inventory, top-3 candidates, and signed boundaries
2. **Manager alignment** — Profile shared with the learner's manager
3. **Clarity** — the learner now has a list of where AI will and won't appear in their work

---

## Assessment criteria

- Inventory has at least 8 tasks across daily/weekly/monthly cadences
- Each task classified for AI fit and data tier
- Top 3 candidates identified with reasoning
- At least 5 boundary items, role-relevant
- Profile sent to manager (or marked "will send by [date]")

---

## Author/facilitator notes

- **The scoring rubric in Activity 9.2 is the magic.** Authors must weight it carefully so learners get realistic ranks (not just "the most frequent task wins").
- **Boundaries are real protection.** Don't undercut the activity by making it optional. Five items minimum.
- **The manager-alignment step is the bridge to the Manager Track.** Make sure the email actually reaches the manager.
