# Module 1: AI for Your Workday

**Pillar:** Awareness · **Duration:** 20 min · **Track:** Foundation Full
**Activity types used:** 2 (multi-model comparison), 1 (single-model prompt)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Rewritten Member Communication; Multi-model Preference Note

---

## Why this module exists

This is the learner's first hands-on AI experience in the course. They will write one prompt, see *three* AI models respond to it side-by-side, and walk away with both a usable banking artifact and an early sense that AI tools are not interchangeable.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the learner will:

1. Send a structured prompt (C-A-T-C) to three AI models simultaneously
2. Compare the three responses and articulate what differs
3. Produce one Rewritten Member Communication they would actually send
4. Capture an initial multi-model preference note

---

## Activities

### Activity 1.1 — Your first prompt, three responses (12 min · type 2)

**Setup (1 min):** "You're going to rewrite a real banker's task — a frustrated member's email reply — using three AI models in parallel. The goal isn't to pick a winner. It's to *see* that AI tools have different voices and different strengths."

**The activity:**
1. Platform presents the messy original draft (one of three role flavors: service follow-up / loan inquiry / branch announcement). Learner picks the flavor closest to their role.
2. Platform shows the C-A-T-C prompt frame:
 - **Context:** "You're a [role] at a $500M community bank..."
 - **Audience:** *(learner picks from preset member types or writes free-form)*
 - **Tone:** *(picker: warm/direct/professional/etc.)*
 - **Constraints:** "Under 120 words. Plain English. End with a clear next step. Do not invent facts."
3. Learner clicks **"Send to all three."** Platform sends the assembled prompt to Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini in parallel.
4. Three responses stream into a side-by-side view.
5. Learner annotates: which response is most warm? Most concise? Most accurate to the facts given? Best banking voice?
6. Learner picks one as the base and may merge a phrase or two from the others.
7. Learner edits the merged draft into a final version.

**Capture:** the original draft, the C-A-T-C prompt, all three model responses, the learner's annotations, the final edited version → saved as `module_01_member_communication.md`.

### Activity 1.2 — Your model preference signal (5 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "After one prompt, you can't make a final judgment. But you can capture a first impression that you'll refine across the course."

**The activity:**
- Platform presents a one-screen reflection prompt:
 - "Of the three models, which produced the response you liked best?"
 - "What made it feel right?"
 - "What made the other two feel less right?"
- Learner types responses; the platform formats them into a Multi-Model Preference Note artifact.

**Capture:** `module_01_multimodel_preference_note.md` — first row of a table that grows across modules 1, 2, 8, 11, 13.

### Activity 1.3 — "Back at the bank" callout (2 min · reading)

> *"At your bank, you won't have parallel access to three models for a single task. The bank's approved tool is most likely **Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat with your work account** (the one with the shield icon for commercial data protection). The skill you just practiced — write a structured prompt, evaluate the response critically — is the same skill you'll apply with one tool."*

Platform shows a simulated screenshot of Copilot Chat with the shield icon highlighted. Learner clicks "Got it" to confirm they understand the bridge from platform → bank.

---

## Daily-use outcomes (the learner walks away with)

1. **A Rewritten Member Communication** they could literally send today (after final review)
2. **A Multi-Model Preference Note** that begins their judgment-building log
3. **Visceral knowledge** that AI tools are not the same, even on identical inputs — a foundation for everything in later modules

---

## Assessment criteria (auto-graded by platform)

Pass criteria:
- All three model responses received and annotated
- Final edited version is materially different from any single model output
- Multi-Model Preference Note has substantive answers (not blank, not one-word)

---

## Author/facilitator notes

- **The first impression is everything.** This is the first activity in the course. Make sure the model responses are calibrated — currently Claude tends warm, ChatGPT tends balanced, Gemini tends concise. If model behaviors shift in quarterly refresh, re-author the activity description.
- **Don't let the platform pick a "winner" for the learner.** The learner's choice is data. Aggregate it across the cohort to inform the bank's tool decisions.
- **Three role flavors only.** Don't add a fourth. Choice fatigue erodes the first-impression value.
