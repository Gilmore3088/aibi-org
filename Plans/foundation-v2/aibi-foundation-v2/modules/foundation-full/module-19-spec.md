# Module 19: Examiner Q&A Practice

**Pillar:** Application · **Duration:** 20 min · **Track:** Foundation Full
**Activity types used:** 4 (adaptive scenario — simulated examiner)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Examiner Q&A Prep Card

---

## Why this module exists

Examiners are starting to ask about AI use in community banks. *"Tell me about your AI training program. How do you ensure staff use AI safely? What governance covers AI tools?"* The bank that has *answers* — backed by training records, policies, and artifacts — is the bank that gets a clean exam. The bank that improvises gives examiners reasons to dig deeper.

Module 19 is a simulated examiner conversation. The learner faces realistic questions, picks responses, sees the consequence, and walks away with a Q&A Prep Card that captures the strongest answers in their own voice.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the learner will:

1. Answer five common examiner questions about AI in plain, calm, factual language
2. Identify the artifact or document that backs each answer
3. Recognize when an answer needs to escalate to compliance or executive
4. Walk away with a Prep Card they can review before any examiner conversation

---

## Activities

### Activity 19.1 — The simulated examiner (15 min · type 4)

**Setup (1 min):** "An OCC (or FDIC, or state) examiner sits down and asks five questions about AI use at your bank. You'll pick how to answer each. The examiner will follow up based on what you say."

**The activity:**

Five branching scenarios, ~3 minutes each.

**Q1: "Tell me about your AI training program."**
- Branches test: Do you confidently describe AiBI-Foundation? Do you reference completion records? Do you stumble? Do you say "we don't really have one" (catastrophic)?
- Best path: name the program (Foundation), describe scope (every employee), point to records (LMS), reference governance owner.

**Q2: "How do you ensure staff don't paste customer NPI into public AI tools?"**
- Branches test: Do you reference the data-tier framework? Do you reference the Five Never-Do's signed by every employee? Do you over-promise? ("We block all public AI tools" — usually false)
- Best path: describe the data-tier framework, the signed never-do's, the technical controls (web-filtering, MDM), and the incident response process if a paste occurs.

**Q3: "Show me your AI use case inventory."**
- Branches test: Do you have one? Is it current? Does it map to the bank's risk taxonomy?
- Best path: reference the rolled-up Personal Prompt Libraries, the Departmental Skill Library if Specialist tier is deployed, and any vendor-AI use registered with TPRM.

**Q4: "How do you handle vendor AI features that touch member data?"**
- Branches test: Do you describe the TPRM process? Do you reference Module 15's Vendor Evaluation Scorecard? Do you wave at "the COO handles that"?
- Best path: name the TPRM process, describe the AI-specific scorecard, point to a recent vendor evaluation.

**Q5: "What's your incident response if AI causes a data event?"**
- Branches test: Do you reference Module 18's Incident Response Checklist? Do you know who to call? Do you have a notification template?
- Best path: walk through the seven steps, name the First-Call List, reference the template.

For each, the examiner's "follow-up question" depends on the learner's answer. A solid answer → light follow-up. A vague answer → drilling follow-up.

**Capture:** the learner's path + best-path answers as the basis of the Prep Card.

### Activity 19.2 — Format your Prep Card (4 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "The Prep Card is for the next time these questions come up — formal exam, internal audit, or an executive asking on the way to the elevator."

**The activity:**

Platform pre-fills the Prep Card with:

- The 5 questions (and likely follow-ups)
- The learner's strongest answer for each (refined)
- The artifact or document that backs each answer
- The escalation point if the learner doesn't have an answer ("for this question, please contact our [compliance officer / model risk owner / etc.]")

Learner edits in their voice and saves.

**Capture:** Examiner Q&A Prep Card → `module_19_examiner_qa_prep_card.md`.

## Daily-use outcomes

1. An **Examiner Q&A Prep Card** the learner reviews before any examiner conversation
2. **Internalized confidence** — the learner has practiced the most common AI questions
3. **A clear escalation map** — the learner knows when to defer and to whom

---

## Assessment criteria

- All 5 questions answered with the platform's best-path or coached to it
- Prep Card has factual answers (not aspirational)
- Each answer references a real artifact or document the bank can produce

---

## Author/facilitator notes

- **The examiner persona must be neutral, professional, neither hostile nor friendly.** Examiners aren't adversaries; they're not friends either.
- **Quarterly refresh:** rotate at least 2 of the 5 questions to reflect evolving examiner focus areas. NIST Cyber AI Profile and Treasury FS AI RMF are increasingly referenced.
- **The "I don't know — let me get our compliance officer" answer is acceptable for many questions.** Don't make the activity teach learners to bluff.
