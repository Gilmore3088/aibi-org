# Module 4: Safe AI Use I — Data and the Five Never-Do's

**Pillar:** Awareness · **Duration:** 25 min · **Track:** Foundation Full
**Activity types used:** 3 (sorting / classification), 4 (adaptive scenario)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Personal Data-Tier Routing Card; Five Never-Do's signed acknowledgment

---

## Why this module exists

Before any further building, the learner must internalize the four data tiers and the five never-do's. This is the *literacy* part of safe AI use — the part every employee in the bank needs, including those who will only complete Foundation Lite.

The activity-driven approach means the learner isn't told the rules; they discover them through sorting and scenario play. By the end they have a routing card that they will reference every time they touch AI.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the learner will:

1. Correctly sort 20 banking artifacts into the four data tiers
2. Apply the five never-do's to scenarios with adaptive feedback
3. Produce a personalized Data-Tier Routing Card for their role
4. Sign acknowledgment of the never-do's

---

## Activities

### Activity 4.1 — The data-tier sort (12 min · type 3)

**Setup (1 min):** "You're going to sort 20 banking artifacts into four buckets: Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted. The platform will tell you when you get one wrong, and why."

**The activity:**

Drag-and-drop interface. Items presented as cards. Drop zones are the four tiers.

Sample items (rotated quarterly):

1. The bank's Q3 press release on community lending
2. A draft email to a member about overdraft fees
3. A list of past-due commercial loans with member names
4. The 2026 strategic plan PowerPoint
5. A BSA/AML alert narrative
6. The bank's posted CD rate sheet
7. A staff performance review
8. A board packet not yet distributed
9. A loan denial letter draft with applicant name and reason
10. An ALCO meeting agenda template (no numbers filled in)
11. A vendor contract draft
12. A teller scheduling spreadsheet
13. A community sponsorship request letter
14. An exam workpaper
15. A wire transfer instruction with member account number
16. The branch-hours announcement for the website
17. An internal SOP on cash handling
18. A new-hire onboarding checklist
19. A loan committee meeting transcript with borrower names
20. A complaint log with three member complaints

**Adaptive feedback:** when learner misclassifies, platform explains:
- "This contains a member name tied to account data → Restricted, not Confidential"
- "This is already published on the bank's website → Public, not Internal"
- "Strategic plans are sensitive even without member data → Confidential"

After each correction, the learner re-classifies.

**Capture:** correctly-sorted final state → forms the basis of the Routing Card.

### Activity 4.2 — Never-Do scenarios (8 min · type 4)

**Setup (1 min):** "Five short scenarios. Each tests one of the five never-do's. Pick what you'd do."

**The activity:**

Five rapid-fire scenarios, one per never-do:

1. *"You're drafting a denial letter. The applicant's name and full SSN are in your notes. You want to use ChatGPT to refine the wording. What do you do?"* — tests *never paste NPI into a public AI tool*
2. *"AI generated a credit decision recommendation. You like it. The borrower meeting is in 10 minutes. What do you do?"* — tests *AI drafts, humans decide*
3. *"AI gave you a polished draft of a regulatory disclosure. The deadline is today. What do you do?"* — tests *review every word*
4. *"You want AI to score your applicants on credit risk. What do you do?"* — tests *no AI for eligibility decisions without approved workflows*
5. *"You used Copilot Chat last month for a workflow. It worked great. You want to do the same thing today. What do you do?"* — tests *don't assume yesterday's approval is today's approval*

For each, learner picks from 3-4 options. Wrong picks branch to a "what would happen" consequence; right picks branch to "yes, here's why."

**Capture:** the learner's pattern of correct/incorrect picks and the platform's reasoning → entry in their Routing Card.

### Activity 4.3 — Sign your card (4 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "Your Routing Card is yours. Sign it. You'll keep it at your desk."

**The activity:**
1. Platform pre-fills the Data-Tier Routing Card with the learner's correctly-sorted tier examples and the five never-do's they just walked through.
2. Learner adds their role-specific tier examples (the platform prompts: "What's a piece of Restricted data you handle weekly?").
3. Learner signs and dates electronically.

**Capture:** signed Data-Tier Routing Card → `module_04_data_tier_routing_card.md`.

## Daily-use outcomes

1. A **signed Data-Tier Routing Card** with the learner's role-specific examples and the five never-do's
2. **Internalized sorting skill** — the learner can classify any new artifact instinctively
3. **The five never-do's, signed** — accountability and a record for compliance

---

## Assessment criteria

- 18 of 20 sort items classified correctly (after feedback iterations)
- Correct answer chosen on at least 4 of 5 never-do scenarios
- Card signed with role-specific examples filled in

---

## Author/facilitator notes

- **The sort is the lesson.** Don't shortcut it. The 20-item set is intentional — fewer leaves gaps, more invites fatigue.
- **Adaptive feedback wording matters.** Never-snarky. Always specific to the item. "This is the strategic plan — even without member data, an unauthorized leak would harm the bank, so it's Confidential."
- **The role-specific fill-in at the end forces transfer to the learner's actual job.** Don't skip it.
- **Quarterly refresh:** rotate the 20 items so the activity stays fresh.
