# Module 6: Talking About AI With Members

**Pillar:** Understanding · **Duration:** 20 min · **Track:** Foundation Full
**Activity types used:** 4 (adaptive scenario — member dialogues), 1 (single-model prompt)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Member-Conversation Script Cards (5)

---

## Why this module exists

Members are starting to ask: *"Is your bank using AI on my account?"* / *"Was this letter written by AI?"* / *"I don't want any AI making decisions about me."* Today, staff improvise answers. Tomorrow, those improvised answers will be grounds for complaint, exit, or worse — bad press in a community where trust is the franchise.

This module gives the learner five rehearsed, accurate, brand-consistent scripts for the most common member-AI conversations. They practice each in a scenario engine until the responses are second-nature.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the learner will:

1. Recognize the five most common member questions about AI
2. Respond to each in a way that is honest, calm, and brand-consistent
3. Distinguish what to disclose from what's appropriate to keep internal
4. Walk away with five Script Cards they keep at the front line

---

## Activities

### Activity 6.1 — The five member conversations (15 min · type 4)

**Setup (1 min):** "Five short scenarios. In each, a member asks you something about AI. Pick how you'd respond. The platform shows you what happens next."

**The activity:**

Five branching scenarios. Each ~3 minutes. Each ends with a captured script card.

**Scenario 1 — "Are you using AI on my account?"**
- Member asks during a routine call. Variants of "yes," "no," "kind of," and "deflection" branch differently.
- Best path: honest, simple ("Yes — for things like fraud monitoring and helping draft communications. A human always reviews anything that affects your account."), then offer to share more.

**Scenario 2 — "Did AI write this letter you sent me?"**
- Member is offended. Branches test whether learner gets defensive, dishonest, or transparent.
- Best path: acknowledge how AI is used at the bank for drafting, confirm a human reviewed and signed, offer to discuss anything that felt wrong about the letter.

**Scenario 3 — "I don't want AI making any decisions about me."**
- Branches test whether learner overcommits ("we won't use any AI for you"), under-commits ("we have to use AI"), or finds the honest middle.
- Best path: clarify which decisions AI assists vs. which it makes (none, in your bank), confirm the member's right to a fully-human path for credit decisions, and document the preference.

**Scenario 4 — "My friend's bank uses AI to make loan decisions. Do you?"**
- Branches test fact-vs-spin balance.
- Best path: explain your bank's actual practice (likely: AI-assisted analysis, human decision), differentiate without disparaging competitors, redirect to what your bank values.

**Scenario 5 — "I think you used AI on the wrong account — your letter mentioned things that weren't about me."**
- This is the *real* hard one. The member is reporting what may be a hallucination or a mis-merge. Branches test whether learner takes it seriously immediately.
- Best path: take it seriously, document immediately, escalate to compliance, follow up promptly. This is a potential GLBA / privacy event.

For each scenario, the learner's choice path is captured along with the platform's rubric assessment.

**Capture:** five Script Cards (one per scenario), each with the learner's chosen best-path responses → `module_06_member_conversation_script_cards.md`.

### Activity 6.2 — Customize for your voice (4 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "The scripts are starting points. Use AI to put them in *your* voice."

**The activity:**
- Platform sends learner's five scripts to a default model (Claude or GPT) with the prompt: *"Refine these scripts so they sound like a [member services rep / branch manager / loan officer] at a $500M community bank with a strong relationship-banking culture. Keep each under 75 words. Keep them honest and direct. Don't add corporate language."*
- Learner reviews, picks favorites, edits.

**Capture:** finalized Script Cards.

---

## Daily-use outcomes

1. **Five Script Cards** for the most common member-AI conversations
2. **Internalized stance** that honest disclosure is the bank's competitive advantage, not a risk
3. **A clear escalation path** for the rare hard cases (Scenario 5)

---

## Assessment criteria

- All five scenarios completed
- Best-path response selected on at least 4 of 5 (or, with feedback, learner re-attempts to find best path)
- Final cards under 75 words each, in role-appropriate voice

---

## Author/facilitator notes

- **The scenarios must be calibrated to *your* bank's actual AI usage.** Author bibles should include a "current bank state" section that adapts the best-path answers (e.g., if the bank doesn't yet use AI for fraud monitoring, Scenario 1's best path needs adjustment).
- **Scenario 5 is the highest-stakes.** Don't compress it. The learner needs to feel the weight.
- **Quarterly refresh:** member sentiment and questions evolve. Review the five scenarios each quarter against actual member feedback.
