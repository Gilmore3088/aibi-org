# Foundation Lite — Module L2: What AI Is, Is Not, and How to Spot a Lie

**Track:** Foundation Lite · **Duration:** 25 min
**Activity types used:** 6 (find the flaw — combined hallucination + cyber awareness lab)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Hallucination Catch Log entry; awareness of AI-augmented threats

---

## Why this module exists

Foundation Lite combines what Foundation Full splits across two modules (Module 2 + parts of Module 5). For a literacy track, the key behaviors are:

1. AI is good at language, bad at facts
2. AI hallucinates with confidence
3. AI now powers convincing phishing, deepfake voices, and document-based attacks
4. The verification habit applies to everything

By the end of L2, a teller or branch staffer recognizes that AI's confidence is not the same as accuracy — and recognizes that AI in the hands of bad actors changes what threats look like.

---

## Activities

### Activity L2.1 — Hallucination Lab (10 min · type 6)

Same as Foundation Full Module 2 Activity 2.1 (trigger a hallucination). The learner sends a known hallucination-prone prompt to multiple models, observes the fabrications, and documents what they saw.

**Capture:** entry in Hallucination Catch Log.

### Activity L2.2 — Spot the AI-augmented phishing (10 min · type 6)

**Setup (1 min):** "Three suspicious messages. Tag each: real / phish / AI-augmented phish. Look for the patterns."

**The activity:**

Three short messages presented:

1. A grammatically clean email from "your CEO" requesting an urgent wire transfer
2. A vendor email with a hidden instruction inside an attached PDF (prompt injection demo)
3. A voice clip of "the regulator" calling about a question (deepfake voice sample)

Learner annotates what's suspicious about each. Platform reveals the AI-augmented attack patterns:
- Perfect grammar at scale (older phishing had errors; AI-generated phishing doesn't)
- Hidden document instructions (prompt injection)
- Voice cloning from publicly available recordings

**Capture:** Personal Threat Awareness Card with the three attack patterns.

### Activity L2.3 — The verify-before-act habit (4 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "When in doubt, verify. The platform will help you build a 30-second habit."

**The activity:**

Learner picks one of:
- "I will always call back through a known number when asked to do anything financial."
- "I will not act on any AI-generated content without a human review."
- "When something feels off, I escalate immediately to my supervisor or compliance."

Plus their own additions. Platform formats into a 3-line "verify-before-act" card.

**Capture:** Verify-Before-Act Card → `module_L2_verify_before_act_card.md`.
