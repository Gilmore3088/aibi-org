# Board Briefing — Module BB1: AI in Banking — What You Need to Know as a Director

**Track:** Board Briefing · **Duration:** 30 min · No prerequisites
**Activity types used:** 4 (adaptive scenario — board-meeting question types), 1 (single-model prompt)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Board AI Vocabulary Card

---

## Why this module exists

Directors need vocabulary, not skill-building. They need to know enough to ask good questions in a board meeting, understand the answers, and exercise governance — without being expected to use AI tools themselves.

Module BB1 walks the director through the kinds of questions that come up in a banking board meeting and produces a one-page Vocabulary Card the director keeps in their board packet.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the director will:

1. Recognize the four major AI-related topics that come up in board meetings (use cases, governance, third-party risk, incident response)
2. Distinguish substance from hype in management's AI presentations
3. Ask three sharp follow-up questions in any AI-related board discussion
4. Walk away with a Board AI Vocabulary Card

---

## Activities

### Activity BB1.1 — Board scenarios (22 min · type 4)

**Setup (1 min):** "Four scenarios from a real community-bank board meeting. Pick how you'd respond as a director."

**The activity:**

Four branching scenarios (~5 min each):

**BB1-1: The CEO presents AI strategy.**
*"Your CEO presents a one-slide AI strategy: 'we are deploying AI across the bank to drive efficiency and improve member experience.' What's your follow-up question?"*
- Wrong path: "Sounds great, what's the budget?" → not governance
- Right path: "Walk me through one specific use case. What data does it touch? Who's reviewing the outputs? How do we know it's safe?"

**BB1-2: The risk officer presents AI risks.**
*"Your CRO presents AI risks at a high level: 'we have a governance framework, we follow OCC and FDIC guidance, we have a vendor management process.' What's your follow-up?"*
- Right path: "Show me the AI use case inventory. How many active uses? What's the highest-risk one? Who's accountable for each?"

**BB1-3: A vendor pitch reaches the board.**
*"Management is recommending a $400K AI-powered LOS upgrade. The vendor pitch made big claims about credit decision automation. What do you do?"*
- Right path: "Has TPRM reviewed this? Has model risk reviewed this? What's the human-in-the-loop design? What's the SOC 2 status? Who would be accountable for outcomes?"

**BB1-4: An incident report reaches the board.**
*"Audit committee chair reports that an employee pasted member NPI into a public AI tool last quarter. Compliance handled it, no member harm. What do you ask?"*
- Right path: "What systemic change? What's the training program? Have we tested whether this could happen again? What's the disclosure standard going forward?"

**Capture:** the director's chosen follow-up questions from each scenario → Board AI Vocabulary Card.

### Activity BB1.2 — Vocabulary Card (7 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "The Card distills what you just learned into a reference you can use in any meeting."

**The activity:**

Platform pre-fills a one-page Board AI Vocabulary Card with:
- The four topic areas (use cases / governance / third-party risk / incident response)
- The 4 sharpest questions from each scenario
- A short glossary: Model Risk, TPRM, Hallucination, Data Tier, Human-in-the-Loop, NPI, Tenant Grounding
- A red-flag list (vague answers, lack of specific names, no use case inventory)

Director edits and saves.

**Capture:** Board AI Vocabulary Card → `module_BB1_board_ai_vocabulary_card.md`.
