# Board Briefing — Module BB2: Governance Questions Every Director Should Ask

**Track:** Board Briefing · **Duration:** 30 min
**Activity types used:** 5 (build & test — question playbook builder)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Director Question Playbook (15 questions)

---

## Why this module exists

BB1 gave the director vocabulary. BB2 produces the *playbook* — 15 sharp governance questions the director can ask in any board meeting, sorted by topic and ranked by priority.

A director with this playbook on the table in a board meeting changes the quality of the conversation. Management knows the questions are coming and prepares better answers.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the director will:

1. Build a personal Question Playbook organized by topic
2. Stress-test each question against management responses
3. Identify which questions to ask in which meeting types (full board, audit committee, risk committee)
4. Walk away with a 15-question Playbook ready for the next meeting

---

## Activities

### Activity BB2.1 — Build the Playbook (15 min · type 5)

**Setup (1 min):** "Build your Playbook by topic. The platform will help you stress-test each question."

**The activity:**

Platform displays five topics. For each, the director picks 3 questions (15 total) from a curated bank of community-bank-relevant questions, with ability to add custom ones.

**Topic 1 — Use Case Inventory & Accountability**
- "Show me the AI use case inventory. How is it maintained?"
- "For our highest-risk AI use, who is the named accountable owner?"
- "What's our process for adding a new AI use case?"
- "Which use cases have direct member impact?"
- "How do we sunset an AI use case?"

**Topic 2 — Governance & Model Risk**
- "Walk me through our AI governance structure. Who chairs it?"
- "How does our Model Risk Management framework apply to AI?"
- "How do we right-size MRM for community-bank scale per OCC 2025-26?"
- "How do we handle AI used by vendors versus AI we use directly?"
- "What's our update cadence on AI policy?"

**Topic 3 — Third-Party Risk & Vendor AI**
- "How does TPRM evaluate AI features in vendor offerings?"
- "What's our approval process when a vendor adds AI to an existing contract?"
- "Do our vendor contracts address AI training data, model providers, and audit access?"
- "Have we reviewed our top 3 critical vendors for AI use?"
- "What's our exit plan if a vendor's AI feature causes a problem?"

**Topic 4 — Workforce, Training, & Culture**
- "How are we training every employee on safe AI use?"
- "What's our approach when an employee uses unauthorized AI tools (shadow AI)?"
- "How do we measure adoption — completion or actual use?"
- "How do we handle AI in employee performance management?"
- "What's our disclosure standard with members about AI use?"

**Topic 5 — Incident Response & Examiner Readiness**
- "Walk me through our AI incident response process."
- "Have we run a tabletop exercise for an AI incident?"
- "What's our member notification standard if AI causes a privacy event?"
- "How prepared are we to answer 'tell me about your AI program' from an examiner?"
- "What recent regulatory developments are we tracking?"

For each chosen question, the platform offers an "expected answer" or "follow-up if vague" hint.

**Capture:** Director Question Playbook with 15 questions → `module_BB2_director_question_playbook.md`.

### Activity BB2.2 — Stress-test against a sample management response (8 min · type 6)

**Setup (1 min):** "Three sample management responses to your questions. Identify which are substantive."

**The activity:**

Three responses presented (synthetic, calibrated to be realistic for a $500M community bank):

1. A solid response with named owners, specific use cases, and clear process
2. A vague response with corporate language and few specifics
3. A defensive response that resists the question

Director annotates each: "substantive / vague / defensive" and writes a one-line follow-up. Platform reveals the patterns.

**Capture:** annotation entries → addendum to the Playbook.

### Activity BB2.3 — Meeting-type assignment (5 min · type 3)

**Setup (1 min):** "Sort your 15 questions by which board meeting they belong in."

**The activity:**

Three drop zones:
- **Full Board** (5 questions)
- **Audit Committee** (5 questions)
- **Risk Committee / Other** (5 questions)

Director sorts. Platform offers calibration ("Use case inventory is usually risk committee territory; member disclosure standards may belong in full board").

**Capture:** sorted Playbook → final saved version.

### Activity BB2.4 — "Take it to the next meeting" callout (1 min · reading)

> *"Your Playbook is ready. Print it, or keep it on your tablet. In your next AI-related board discussion, pick three questions from the relevant topic. The other directors will follow your lead. The CEO and management will prepare better answers next time."*

## Daily-use outcomes

1. A **Director Question Playbook** with 15 questions sorted by topic and meeting type
2. **Pattern recognition** for substantive vs. vague management responses
3. **Three questions ready** for the next AI-related board meeting

---

## Assessment criteria

- 15 questions selected across all 5 topics
- All three sample responses correctly tagged
- Questions sorted into meeting types
- Director's voice apparent in any custom additions

---

## Board Briefing track completion

Two modules complete (~60 min). The director receives:
- AiBI Board Briefing certification
- Board AI Vocabulary Card
- Director Question Playbook

The board chair / corporate secretary receives notification (with director permission) and can elect to share the Vocabulary Card with the full board.
