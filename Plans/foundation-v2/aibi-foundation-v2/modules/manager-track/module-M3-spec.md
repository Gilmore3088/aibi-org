# Manager Track — Module M3: Spotting Misuse and Closing the Loop

**Track:** Manager · **Duration:** 30 min
**Activity types used:** 7 (tabletop simulation), 4 (adaptive scenario)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Escalation Decision Card

---

## Why this module exists

Coaching is the first response. But some situations need escalation, documentation, and closing the loop with compliance, IT, or HR. Module M3 trains the manager to recognize when a situation has crossed into that territory and what to do.

The activity uses tabletop simulation — the same format as Foundation Full Module 18 — but from the manager's perspective.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the manager will:

1. Distinguish coaching situations from escalation situations
2. Walk through an AI-related incident from the manager's seat
3. Identify the right escalation path and timing
4. Produce an Escalation Decision Card

---

## Activities

### Activity M3.1 — Three escalation tabletops (24 min · type 7)

**Setup (1 min):** "Three situations involving your team. Walk through each. The platform will show consequences."

**The activity:**

Three tabletops (~7 min each):

**Tabletop M3-1: The NPI paste.**
*"You learn (through the team member's voluntary disclosure) that they pasted a member's name + account + balance into ChatGPT yesterday. They're asking you for guidance."*
- Walk-through: containment / scope / immediate escalation / member-impact assessment / corrective action / documentation
- Right path: thank them for disclosure (preserves future disclosure); escalate to compliance immediately; do not punish the disclosure; document; loop in IT for any technical containment; corrective action focused on workflow gap, not the person

**Tabletop M3-2: The discovered shadow AI tool.**
*"During a 1:1, you discover one of your direct reports has been using a personal Claude account on their phone for member email drafting for the past three months. They claim they always strip the member name first."*
- Walk-through: scope / verification of claims / GLBA implications / vendor risk / data-handling audit / corrective action
- Right path: serious but not punitive; verify the "strip first" claim through review; escalate to compliance and IT; document; reinforce approved tools; check whether approved tools are inadequate (a real possibility in many community banks)

**Tabletop M3-3: The vendor pitch attendance.**
*"A direct report attended a vendor pitch for an AI-powered LOS feature. The vendor's pitch had hallucination risk and weak data residency answers. The direct report was impressed and is recommending the bank purchase. Your CEO is interested."*
- Walk-through: ground the conversation in the Module 15 scorecard / loop in TPRM / arrange a deeper vendor evaluation / coach the direct report on Module 15 patterns
- Right path: don't dismiss the direct report's enthusiasm; channel it through the proper TPRM lens; produce a structured scorecard that the CEO can read; escalate to TPRM for formal review; coach the direct report after on the questions they didn't ask

**Capture:** the manager's path through each tabletop + the platform's rubric.

### Activity M3.2 — Build your Escalation Decision Card (5 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "When in doubt, escalate. The Card helps you decide quickly."

**The activity:**

Platform pre-fills an Escalation Decision Card with:
- The five lenses (from Module 7): Model Risk, Third-Party Risk, Fair Lending, BSA/AML, Cybersecurity, Governance
- The First-Call List (carried from Module 7)
- Decision criteria for each tier:
 - Coaching only
 - Document and coach
 - Escalate to compliance / IT / HR / TPRM
 - Immediate escalation + incident response activation

Manager customizes with their bank's specific contacts and saves.

**Capture:** Escalation Decision Card → `module_M3_escalation_decision_card.md`.

## Daily-use outcomes

1. **Tabletop muscle memory** for three common manager-level AI situations
2. **An Escalation Decision Card** with criteria, contacts, and timing
3. **Internalized principle** — disclosure is never punished; misuse is corrected; pattern issues are escalated

---

## Assessment criteria

- All three tabletops completed
- Right-path actions chosen on at least 2 of 3 (with feedback iteration on misses)
- Escalation Decision Card customized to the manager's bank context
