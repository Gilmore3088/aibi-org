# Manager Track — Module M1: Coaching Your Team's AI Use

**Track:** Manager · **Duration:** 30 min
**Prerequisite:** Foundation Full (or Foundation Lite + 6 months supervisory experience)
**Activity types used:** 4 (adaptive scenario — 1:1 coaching role-play), 1 (single-model prompt)
**Daily-use outcomes:** Coaching Conversation Pack (5 starter conversations)

---

## Why this module exists

Foundation teaches the individual. The supervisor is invisible in the Foundation curriculum. That's a gap, because adoption fails when managers don't know how to manage it.

Module M1 gives the manager realistic 1:1 coaching scenarios — the conversations they will actually have with their team about AI use — and produces a Coaching Conversation Pack the manager keeps.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end, the manager will:

1. Coach a direct report through five common AI-related conversations
2. Distinguish coaching from policing
3. Recognize the signs of overuse, underuse, or misuse
4. Walk away with a Coaching Conversation Pack ready for use

---

## Activities

### Activity M1.1 — Five coaching scenarios (24 min · type 4)

**Setup (1 min):** "Five 1:1 conversations you might have with a direct report. Pick how you'd open each."

**The activity:**

Five branching scenarios (~5 min each):

**M1-1: The Foundation graduate who isn't applying it.**
*"Your direct report completed Foundation 60 days ago. Their Personal Prompt Library has 5 entries from the course but none have been used since. How do you open the conversation?"*
- Wrong path: "You're not using what you learned" → defensive
- Right path: "I'm curious how the course is showing up in your work — what's been useful?" → opens dialogue

**M1-2: The over-user.**
*"Your direct report is using AI for everything, including a member condolence note that came back generic. The member noticed. How do you address it?"*
- Right path: focus on the specific incident, the pattern, the boundary the member crossed (sensitive communications), connect to their AI Work Profile boundaries

**M1-3: The shadow AI user.**
*"You catch a direct report using ChatGPT (personal account) on their personal phone for bank work. They say 'I get it done faster.' How do you handle it?"*
- Right path: zero tolerance on Restricted-tier paste; coaching on what *approved* tools provide; check whether bank tools are inadequate (a real possibility); document and escalate per policy

**M1-4: The skeptic.**
*"Your direct report is openly resistant to AI use. They think the bank is taking on risk for marginal benefit. How do you have a useful conversation?"*
- Right path: honor the skepticism (they're not wrong about risk); ask what they'd want to see; share examples of safe high-value use; don't pressure adoption — invite curiosity

**M1-5: The hidden incident.**
*"Your direct report mentions in a 1:1 that they 'almost' pasted member info into ChatGPT last week. They didn't. How do you handle it?"*
- Right path: thank them for telling you; treat it as a near-miss worth documenting; surface the workflow gap that led there; refresh the never-do's; don't punish the disclosure (would prevent future disclosures)

For each, the manager picks an opening and the report responds. After 3–4 exchanges, the platform shows the rubric and the best path.

**Capture:** five Coaching Conversation Cards (one per scenario) → Coaching Conversation Pack.

### Activity M1.2 — Format your Pack (5 min · type 1)

**Setup (1 min):** "The Pack is what you reach for when one of these conversations actually happens."

**The activity:**

Platform pre-fills the Coaching Conversation Pack with the manager's chosen best-path openings, the principles, and the escalation triggers. Manager edits in their voice and saves.

**Capture:** Coaching Conversation Pack → `module_M1_coaching_conversation_pack.md`.

---

## Daily-use outcomes

1. A **Coaching Conversation Pack** with 5 starter conversations
2. **Internalized stance** — coaching, not policing
3. **Recognition** of the five most common manager-team AI patterns

---

## Assessment criteria

- All five scenarios completed
- Best-path or coached-to-best-path on at least 4 of 5
- Pack saved with manager's voice
