## Part 3: The Five Never-Do's

I commit to never:

- [ ] Pasting customer NPI or PII (account numbers, SSNs, balances, full loan files, member names tied to account data) into a public AI tool — including screenshots that contain that data.
- [ ] Letting AI make a final decision that affects a member. AI drafts. AI suggests. A human approves the final action — every time.
- [ ] Copying AI output into a regulatory filing or member-facing document without reviewing every word.
- [ ] Using AI to evaluate a person's eligibility for a product or rate unless my bank has explicitly approved that workflow under its model risk management program.
- [ ] Assuming what worked yesterday is approved today. I will check before pasting if I'm unsure.

## Part 4: My pre-flight check (use every time)

Before pasting anything into an AI tool, I will ask:

1. **What tier is this data?** ( Public · Internal · Confidential · Restricted )
2. **Is the tool I'm using approved for that tier?** ( Yes · No · Not sure → stop and ask )
3. **Have I stripped any NPI/PII I don't need to send?** ( Yes · N/A )
4. **Will a human review the output before it leaves the bank or affects a member?** ( Yes · No → stop )
5. **Could I defend this use to an examiner if asked?** ( Yes · No → stop )

If any answer is "No" or "Not sure" — I stop and ask my manager or compliance contact.

---

## Part 5: My escalation triggers

I will escalate to my manager or compliance immediately if:

- [ ] I accidentally pasted NPI/PII into a public AI tool
- [ ] I notice an AI tool returning output that looks like another customer's information
- [ ] A vendor demonstrates an AI feature that touches member data and I don't see it on our approved tools list
- [ ] AI output for a regulatory filing contains something I cannot verify
- [ ] I'm asked to use AI in a way that doesn't match this checklist
