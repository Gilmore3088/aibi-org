## The four AI topics in board meetings

### 1. Use cases & accountability

**The question that matters:**

> *"Walk me through one specific use case. What data does it touch? Who's reviewing the outputs? How do we know it's safe?"*

### 2. Governance & risk

**The question that matters:**

> *"Show me the AI use case inventory. How many active uses? What's the highest-risk one? Who's accountable for each?"*

### 3. Vendor / third-party AI

**The question that matters:**

> *"Has TPRM reviewed this? What's the human-in-the-loop design? What's the SOC 2 status?"*

### 4. Incident response

**The question that matters:**

> *"What systemic change? What's the training program? Have we tested whether this could happen again?"*

## Glossary — terms to recognize

| Term | Plain-English meaning |
|---|---|
| **LLM** (Large Language Model) | An AI trained to predict next words from patterns. The technology behind ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. |
| **Hallucination** | When AI generates a confident but false answer. Structural to how LLMs work; cannot be eliminated, only managed. |
| **Data tier** | A bank's classification of data (Public / Internal / Confidential / Restricted). AI use must match the tier. |
| **NPI** | Non-Public Personal Information — member account data, balances, SSN, etc. Highest-tier data; never goes into a public AI tool. |
| **Tenant grounding** | When AI tools can use your bank's documents (in your tenant) without exposing them externally. Generally available with paid M365 Copilot. |
| **TPRM** | Third-Party Risk Management — the process for evaluating vendors, including their AI features. |
| **Model Risk** | The risk that an AI tool's outputs are wrong or biased. Governed by SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12 / OCC 2025-26 community-bank flexibility. |
| **Human-in-the-loop** | A design where AI assists but a human always makes the final decision. The core safety pattern. |
| **Shadow AI** | Staff using personal AI accounts for bank work, outside approved tools. A common risk. |

## Red flags in management answers

- Vague answers with corporate language ("we are leveraging AI to drive efficiency")
- Lack of specific names or accountable owners
- No use case inventory available
- "We are not really using AI" (likely untrue at the staff level — surface what's there)
- "All AI is risky / all AI is safe" — neither is correct
- Pressure to "move fast" without governance review
