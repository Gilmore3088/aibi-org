## The four advanced techniques

### 1. Role assignment

Tell the AI who to *be*.

> *Example I'll use:* "You are a [role] at a $500M community bank. You are reviewing this draft for [purpose]."

**My role-assignment line:** _______________________________________________

### 2. Examples (few-shot)

Show, don't just tell. Provide one or two examples of good output.

> *Example I'll use:* "Here are two prior examples of [type of output] in our bank's voice. Apply the same voice."

**My example-paste pattern:** _______________________________________________

### 3. Step-by-step instructions

For multi-part tasks, list each step.

> *Example I'll use:* "Please: 1. [step]. 2. [step]. 3. [step]. Do not invent details."

**My step-list pattern:** _______________________________________________

### 4. Iterative refinement

Don't expect perfection on prompt one. Treat it as a conversation.

> *Three follow-ups I'll always have ready:*
> - "What's missing from that draft?"
> - "Give me three alternative versions: more formal, more casual, and shorter."
> - "What might a regulator or examiner question about this language?"

## Reminders

- **AI is good at language. AI is bad at facts.** Verify any specific claim.
- **Strip member NPI before pasting.** Module 3 rules apply to every prompt.
- **The strongest prompts often *narrow* what AI is being asked to do.**
- **The first prompt is the start of a conversation, not the final answer.**
