## Why this exists

AI can clone a voice from 30 seconds of audio. Audio alone is no longer sufficient verification for a financial instruction. This protocol defines what *is* sufficient.

---

## The four rules

### Rule 1 — Always callback through a known number

Never use the number that called you. Use the number you have on file from a prior verified relationship — the bank directory, the member's CIF record, the vendor's contract.

### Rule 2 — Out-of-band confirmation for any financial instruction

If a voice asks for a wire, a transfer, system access, or a release of funds:
- Confirm through a *different* channel (text, email, in-person, internal Teams)
- The channel must be one you separately know is the person's

### Rule 3 — Multi-person approval thresholds

- Wires above $______ require two voices on different lines, both verified per Rules 1 and 2
- System access requests require a Teams confirmation from the named requester
- After-hours instructions require additional escalation: ___________________

## Tells of a deepfake voice

- Slight monotone or unnatural rhythm
- Lack of audible breath or filler words
- Background that's "too clean" — no ambient noise
- Subtle phonetic artifacts on certain consonants
- The caller resists callback or out-of-band confirmation
- Unusual urgency
