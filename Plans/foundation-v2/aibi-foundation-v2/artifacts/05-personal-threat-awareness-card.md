## Threat 1 — Prompt Injection (in documents)

**What it is:** Hidden instructions in an attached document that the AI follows.

**Where it shows up:**
- Vendor proposals
- Member-submitted documents
- Files from any source you don't fully trust

**My defense:**
- Use the defensive system prompt (Module 5 artifact)
- If the AI flags suspicious content, escalate to IT/security
- Never let AI act autonomously on a document

---

## Threat 2 — Deepfake Voice

**What it is:** A cloned voice on the phone — your CEO, a regulator, a vendor — generated from publicly available recordings.

**Where it shows up:**
- Urgent wire requests
- System access requests
- Member-impact instructions
- Anything financial requested by phone

**My defense:**
- Voice-Verification Protocol (Module 5 artifact)
- Always callback through a known number
- Out-of-band confirmation for any financial instruction

---

## Threat 3 — AI-Augmented Phishing

**What it is:** Phishing emails generated at scale by AI — grammatically perfect, convincingly personal, plausibly relevant.

**Where it shows up:**
- "From your CEO" emails with urgency
- Vendor "invoices" with hidden instructions
- "Regulator" requests with authority pressure

**My defense:**
- Verify sender via separate channel
- Treat urgency itself as a red flag
- Forward suspicious messages to IT/security
- Never act on email-only instructions for anything material
