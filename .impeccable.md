## Design Context

Source: Plans/aibi-designer-brief.html (canonical), user session 2026-04-15.

### Users
Community bank and credit union executives — CEOs, CFOs, COOs, compliance
officers, and board members at FDIC-insured institutions typically under
$10B in assets. They arrive skeptical of AI hype, protective of their
members' data, and acutely aware of their regulatory obligations. They are
not technologists. They evaluate everything through: "is this safe for my
institution, can my people actually do this, and can I justify the cost to
my board?" Their context is a Tuesday morning at a desk with 4 browser
tabs open, not a keynote stage.

### Brand Personality
**Authoritative. Grounded. Human.**

- Authoritative: speaks with the confidence of someone who has read the
  regulations, run the numbers, and delivered the results. Never hedges.
- Grounded: every claim traces to a named source. Every recommendation
  assumes a regulated institution.
- Human: behind the frameworks and the data, there is a teller on a
  Tuesday morning. The mission is giving people who care about their work
  a new set of tools.

Voice: declarative, specific, institutional. Not academic, not casual,
not startup. "We turn bankers into builders" — not "empowering digital
transformation across the financial services ecosystem."

### Emotional Goals
1. **Authority + Trust** — "This institution knows what they are doing.
   I am in safe hands." Like walking into a well-run boardroom.
2. **Aspiration + Pride** — "My institution can lead on this. We are
   not behind — we are positioned to move."

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Ancient wisdom meets modern tech."** Parchment and terracotta.
Institutional authority without startup flavor. The visual language of a
boardroom briefing document, not a SaaS landing page. Think: the weight
of a legal brief on premium paper.

**References:** McKinsey / Oliver Wyman (consulting authority, clean,
minimal, content-first, heavy serif typography). The Economist / Financial
Times (editorial authority, parchment tones, data-rich, institutional
gravitas). Roman architectural details — arcs, circles, ruled lines.

**Anti-references:** Nothing that looks like a fintech startup. No
gradients with purple-to-teal. No abstract geometric blobs. No stock
photos. No emoji. Avoid the visual language of vendor conference booths,
core system sales decks, and "digital transformation" stock photography.
Should look like it belongs in a boardroom, not on a tradeshow floor.

**Theme:** Light mode only. The warm palette IS the brand.

### Color System

**Primary palette:**
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| `--color-linen` | #f9f6f0 | Page background |
| `--color-parch` | #f5f0e6 | Card backgrounds |
| `--color-parch-dark` | #ede6d8 | Hover states, section bands |
| `--color-ink` | #1e1a14 | Primary text, headlines |
| `--color-slate` | #6b6355 | Secondary text |
| `--color-dust` | #9b9085 | Captions, metadata |

**Signal colors (pillar system):**
| Token | Hex | Usage Rule |
|-------|-----|------------|
| `--color-terra` | #b5512e | Primary brand signal. CTAs, borders, labels. Used throughout. |
| `--color-terra-light` | #c96a43 | Hover states, secondary terra. |
| `--color-terra-pale` | #f0c4ab | Background tints, selection highlights, tags. |
| `--color-sage` | #4a6741 | **Pillar A (Accessible) ONLY.** Never as a general accent. |
| `--color-cobalt` | #2d4a7a | **Pillar B (Boundary-Safe) and security band ONLY.** |
| `--color-error` | #9b2226 | Error states, Starting Point tier. |

**Color discipline is non-negotiable.** Using sage outside Pillar A or
cobalt outside Pillar B destroys the three-pillar visual grammar.

### Typography

| Typeface | Use | Weights |
|----------|-----|---------|
| Cormorant Garamond | All headlines, subheadlines, hero subtitle. Italic for warmth and emphasis — never for error states. | 300, 400, 600, 700 |
| Cormorant SC | ALL section labels, nav items, designation names (AiBI-P, AiBI-S, AiBI-L), the seal wordmark. | 400, 500, 600 |
| DM Sans | Body copy, UI elements, buttons, captions, all interface text. | 300, 400, 500, 600 |
| DM Mono | ALL numbers, prices, financial data, score ring, stat band, ROI output, AiBI seal initials. | 400, 500 |

**Italic rules:** Italic Cormorant is used intentionally for warmth and
authority. Use for: key phrases in subheadlines, section subtitles,
pull quotes, and card body text where a contemplative tone is needed.
Never italicize DM Sans body copy — italics there signal error state.

### Spacing System
Scale: 4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 20 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 60 / 88 px

- **Max content width:** 1200px, centered, 60px horizontal padding (24px mobile)
- **Section padding:** 56–80px top/bottom desktop, 48px mobile
- **Card gap:** 24px standard
- **Border radius:** 2–4px structural. Never >4px. Circles for marks/icons only.
- **Breakpoint:** Mobile at 1000px. Desktop-first, mobile graceful degradation.

### Component Specs (from designer brief)

**Buttons:**
- All: uppercase, 11px, DM Sans 600, letter-spacing 1.2px, 2px border-radius
- Primary (btn-terra): solid terra bg, linen text, 13px 28px padding
- Secondary (btn-outline): transparent bg, border color, ink text → hover terra
- CTA reversed (btn-cream): linen bg, terra text (on terra backgrounds only)
- Ghost reversed (btn-lo): transparent, 35% white border (on dark backgrounds)

**Cards:**
- Standard: parch bg, terra border 1px, 3px radius. Top accent: 2px gradient terra→transparent
- Pillar: parch bg, large background number opacity .05, top accent 3px in pillar color
- Certification: parch bg, bottom accent 2px terra (hover reveal via scaleX). Hover: translateY(-3px)
- Engagement: linen bg, no accent. Hover: background darkens to parch

**Navigation:**
- Sticky, linen bg 97% opacity, 70px height, terra bottom border 1px
- Seal + wordmark lockup left, nav links Cormorant SC 12px center, CTA right

**The AiBI Seal:**
- Circle with fine stroke border (1.5–2px). "AiBI" in Cormorant SC.
- Should feel like stamped wax, not a software icon.
- Color variants: terra/linen, white/terra, white/ink, terra/parch
- Minimum clear space: width of the letter "A" on all sides

### Design Principles

1. **Content is the design.** The copy does the selling. The design
   creates authority through typography, spacing, and restraint — not
   decoration. If a section looks empty, the copy needs to be stronger.

2. **Every number earns its place.** No unsourced statistics. Every
   figure traces to a named publication. DM Mono for all numerical data.
   Tabular-nums so columns align.

3. **Institutional, not promotional.** The site should feel like a
   consulting firm's client-facing materials — not a marketing funnel.
   No animated gradients, no floating particles, no "limited time"
   urgency. The urgency comes from the data.

4. **Pillar discipline is visual grammar.** Sage = Accessible. Cobalt =
   Boundary-Safe. Terra = brand/Capable. Using them interchangeably
   confuses the reader even if they cannot articulate why.

5. **Accessible by default.** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Visible focus
   rings. Skip-to-content link. Sufficient contrast. No information
   conveyed by color alone.

### What NOT to Do
- No gradients or glass effects (except subtle card top-border treatment)
- No drop shadows — elevation through color contrast only
- No rounded corners >4px on structural elements
- No emoji in any context
- No generic icon libraries (Heroicons, Lucide, etc.)
- No stock photography
- No animations that serve decoration rather than function
- No "AI-powered" / "smart" / "intelligent" badge language
- No dark mode (the warm palette IS the brand)
- No uppercase sans-serif for body text
- No blue as a primary accent — cobalt is pillar-reserved only
- No backgrounds darker than ink on light surfaces
