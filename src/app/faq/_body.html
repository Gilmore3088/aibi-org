01About the institute

Who built this and why.

An independent education company. No model partnerships, no resellers, no "powered by" logos. Built by a banker because bankers need to understand AI — not be sold it.

Q.01

Are you a vendor? Are you trying to sell us a model?

No. The institute is independent — no model partnerships, no reseller agreements, no "powered by" anything. We teach Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini side-by-side, and we tell you when each one is the wrong choice. The only thing we sell is the program itself.

+
Q.02

Who runs it?

A working banker — credit and operations background — built and runs the curriculum. Reviewer staff are working bankers from credit, deposit, BSA, and ops. There is no engineering team trying to learn banking on the fly.

+
Q.03

How is this different from existing AI training?

Most AI training is either (a) a vendor demo dressed as a class, or (b) a generic "prompt engineering" survey. This is neither. It's banking-specific work product — credit memos, BSA narratives, vendor exception letters, board summaries — reviewed by working bankers. You leave with artifacts, not just slides.

+
02The credential

What it is, what it isn't.

A proficiency standard, not a regulatory qualification. The vocabulary aligns with how examiners speak — so when you bring AI work into committee, your language matches.

Q.04

Will my examiner accept the credential?

The credential isn't a regulatory qualification — it's a proficiency standard. But the curriculum and assessment vocabulary map directly to SR 11-7, TPRM, ECOA / Reg B, and the AIEOG AI Lexicon. The point is that when you bring AI work into committee, your language matches what your examiner expects to hear.

+
Q.05

Foundations vs. Practitioner — what's the difference?

AiBI-Foundations is a self-paced 12-module course that establishes shared vocabulary, governance literacy, and supervised hands-on practice. AiBI-Practitioner is the cohort track — same modules, plus reviewer-graded artifacts, peer review, and a quarterly readout you can take to your board or examiner.

+
Q.06

How is the credential verified?

Every credential carries a public verification URL with an issue date, the holder's permitted name, and the cohort number. Anyone can verify it without an account at aibankinginstitute.com/verify. Credentials don't expire, but a re-attestation is recommended every 24 months as the curriculum updates.

+
03Who it's for

Line bankers and the people next to them.

Lenders, deposit teams, BSA, ops, and L&D leaders. Nothing to install. No code. If you're a developer the curriculum will feel slow — and that's intentional.

Q.07

Is this for technical staff or for line bankers?

Line bankers, ops, lenders, deposit teams, BSA, and L&D leaders. There's nothing to install and no code. If you're a developer, the curriculum will feel slow — that's intentional. The institute is for the people whose day is actually being changed.

+
Q.08

What's the time commitment?

Foundations: about 18 hours, completed at your own pace. Practitioner cohort: 12 weeks at roughly 3 hours/week, with a single live 60-minute review session each fortnight. Most learners do the work during normal admin time, not after hours.

+
Q.09

Do I need permission from IT or compliance to start?

No to start the assessment. For the Practitioner cohort we recommend a quick conversation with your model risk and infosec leads — the curriculum includes a short briefing memo template you can send them, written in their language.

+
04How it works

Reviewed work. Margin notes. No quizzes.

Every module ends in an artifact. You submit it. A working banker reads it and leaves margin notes the way a senior would. You revise. That's the loop.

Q.10

What does "reviewed work" actually mean?

Every module ends in an artifact — a brief, a memo, a policy revision, a redacted exception letter. You submit it. A working banker (credit, deposit, ops) reads it and leaves margin notes the way a senior would. You revise. That's the loop. There are no multiple-choice quizzes.

+
Q.11

Does the assessment require my email or company info?

No. You can take all twelve questions, see your tier, and read your written diagnostic without entering anything. We ask for an email only if you want the starter artifact and follow-up Brief delivered.

+
Q.12

Can I bring my own documents?

Yes — and we encourage it. Bring redacted versions of credit memos, board summaries, vendor exception letters, or BSA narratives. The activities are designed to be reframed around your real artifacts. You leave with a working draft you can take to next week's committee.

+
05Cohorts & pricing

What it costs and what you get.

Per-seat pricing, published. No sales cycle. Cohorts of 5+ get a private review track, peer matching, and a quarterly readout for your board or examiner.

Q.13

Can we run this for a cohort or whole team?

Yes — see "For institutions." Cohorts of 5+ get a private review track, peer activity matching, and a quarterly readout for your board or examiner. Pricing scales with seats; we publish per-seat rates rather than running a sales cycle.

+
Q.14

What's the per-seat price?

Foundations: $890/seat. Practitioner (cohort, reviewed work): $2,400/seat. Cohorts of 10+ are 15% off; 25+ are 25% off. There is no upcharge for the quarterly readout — it's part of every cohort.

+
Q.15

Refund or satisfaction policy?

Full refund within the first two weeks of any cohort, no questions asked. After two weeks, refunds are pro-rated against modules not yet reviewed. We don't run "money-back if you don't get a job" gimmicks — this isn't that kind of program.

+
06Security & governance

What we do with your work.

Submitted artifacts stay yours. Reviewers see redacted versions. Nothing is used to train models — ours or anyone else's.

Q.16

Where does my submitted work live?

In an isolated, encrypted reviewer workspace. Visible only to your assigned reviewer and the program lead. Deleted from active systems 90 days after credential issuance; retained in cold backup for 7 years for credential audit, then destroyed.

+
Q.17

Do you train models on our work?

No. Submitted artifacts are never used as training data — by us, by the model providers we teach across, or by anyone else. The activities you do inside Claude / ChatGPT / Gemini are governed by each provider's enterprise data terms; we walk you through what each one means before you use it.

+
Q.18

SOC 2? BSA / FFIEC alignment?

SOC 2 Type II audit complete; report available under NDA. Curriculum and assessment language map to FFIEC IT Handbook, SR 11-7, ECOA / Reg B, and the AIEOG AI Lexicon. We publish a vendor due-diligence packet that most community banks can drop straight into their TPRM file.
