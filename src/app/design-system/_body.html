House voice · sample

Voice

Editorial, not promotional

  • Short sentences. Few adjectives. Never “supercharge,” “unlock,” or “revolutionize.”
  • Use “you” in the second person. Avoid “users.”
  • Numbers earn their place — don't pad with stats that prove nothing.
  • No exclamation points. Ever.
  • No emoji unless quoting someone using one.

Use color for emphasis, never for decoration. The accent is gold; the warning is oxblood; the body is parchment.

Usage
  • Body text on Paper or BG, never Parch (insufficient contrast).
  • Gold for inline emphasis, kicker labels, current-state indicators, primary CTAs that aren't ink.
  • Oxblood is for destructive / late / failed states only. Never for marketing.
  • Don't use accent on accent (gold text on gold backing). Use ink on gold or paper on gold.

Family roles

  • Newsreader — titles, ledes, quotes, the brand wordmark.
  • Geist — body, UI labels, buttons (when not mono).
  • JetBrains Mono — kickers, metadata, code, version & status pills.
Rules
  • Italics signal voice — they are not for emphasis. Use Newsreader italic for ledes, quotes, and the trailing clause of section titles.
  • Caps + 0.16–0.20em letter-spacing only on mono. Don't track sans.
  • Body line-height 1.55. Display line-height 0.95–1.1.
  • Never set Geist or Newsreader at letter-spacing > 0.06em.
  • Never set mono < 9px.

21 VOICE & COPY

Voice & copy.

Editorial first. Promotional never. Specific over clever.

Do

Twelve modules, twelve real artifacts. By the end, you'll have memos a credit committee will read without flinching.

Don't

Unlock the power of AI to revolutionize your banking workflows with cutting-edge tools that 10x your productivity!

Heuristics
  • Lead with the artifact, not the tool.
  • Numbers are concrete (12 modules, 28 minutes), not vague (many, several).
  • Names of real banking objects: memo, letter, committee, draw, charge-off.
  • No "AI-powered." It is AI. We know.
  • No "users," "leverage," "synergy," or sentence-case CTAs.
