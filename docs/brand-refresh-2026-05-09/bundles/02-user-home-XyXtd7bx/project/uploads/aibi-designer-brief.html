Brand & Visual System

For the designer responsible for visual identity, UI components, and all branded assets.
01 · Aesthetic Direction

Ancient wisdom
meets modern tech.

This is the single most important brief you'll receive for this brand. Everything visual should feel like it belongs in both a 200-year-old bank and a serious technology firm — simultaneously. Not retro. Not startup. Something that earns trust the moment you see it.

Feel
Parchment and terracotta. The warmth of old documents and institutional authority. Architectural ornament without decoration for decoration's sake. Think: the weight of a legal brief on premium paper.
References
Oliver Wyman financial services practice materials. McKinsey banking sector reports. The Economist's clean authority. Roman architectural details — arcs, circles, ruled lines. Manuscript illumination without the gold leaf.
Counter-References
Nothing that looks like a fintech startup. No gradients with purple-to-teal. No abstract geometric blobs. No stock photos of diverse people pointing at screens. No emoji anywhere in the brand system.

Specifically avoid the visual language of vendor conference booths, core system sales decks, and "digital transformation" stock photography. AiBI should look like it belongs in a boardroom, not on a tradeshow floor.
Visual Do's
Ruled lines, hairlines, and ornamental separators
Circular seal/mark motifs with fine border
Warm cream backgrounds — never pure white
Monospaced type for numbers and data
Generous white space with intentional density contrast
Terracotta as the singular signal color
Italic serif for emphasis — it earns its weight
Visual Don'ts
Gradients (except the subtle top-border treatment on cards)
Drop shadows (elevation is implied through color, not shadow)
Icons that look like they came from an icon library
Rounded corners more than 4px (keeps authority)
Uppercase sans-serif for body text
Blue as a primary or accent color — cobalt is reserved for security contexts only
Any background darker than the ink color on light surfaces

Color Discipline
Terra is the ONLY brand signal color used outside of pillar-specific contexts. Sage is only used when discussing Pillar A (Accessible). Cobalt is only used when discussing Pillar B (Boundary-Safe). Using them interchangeably destroys the three-pillar visual system. The security band on the website is cobalt for this reason — it immediately signals which pillar is being discussed.
03 · Typography

Three typefaces.
Each with a defined job.

Type Stack
Display / Headings: Cormorant (Google Fonts) — weights 300, 400, 600, 700. Italic variants used intentionally for emphasis and warmth.

Small Caps / Labels: Cormorant SC (Google Fonts) — weights 400, 500, 600. Used for ALL section labels, navigation items, designation names (AiBI-P, AiBI-S, AiBI-L), and the AiBI wordmark itself.

Body / UI: DM Sans (Google Fonts) — weights 300, 400, 500, 600. Clean, modern, highly legible at small sizes. Used for all body copy, buttons, and UI elements.

Data / Code: DM Mono (Google Fonts) — weights 400, 500. Used for ALL numbers, prices, hex codes, financial data, and the AiBI seal initials.

Rules for Italic Usage
Italic Cormorant is used intentionally for warmth and authority — never as an afterthought. Use it for: key phrases in subheadlines that need emphasis ("your people will actually use it"), section subtitles in the hero, pull quotes, and card body text where a contemplative tone is needed. Never italicize body copy (DM Sans) — italics there signal error state.
04 · The AiBI Mark

The seal.
What it needs to be.

The mark direction is established in the HTML prototype — a circular seal with fine border, Cormorant SC initials, institutional weight. The designer's job is to produce the production-ready vector package.

Mark Specification
Form: Circle. Fine stroke border (1.5–2px at production size). Not a badge, not a pill — a seal. The weight should suggest something stamped into wax, not a software icon.

Interior: "AiBI" in Cormorant SC. The "Ai" should feel slightly smaller or lighter than the "BI" to preserve the internal visual hierarchy — AI Banking Institute, not AI-Bi. Alternatively: the full four letters at equal weight in SC is also acceptable if it reads cleanly.

Color variants required: Terra on linen (primary) · White on terra (reversed for CTA band) · White on ink (for dark backgrounds/footer) · Terra on parchment (for document use)
Wordmark + Seal Lockup
Primary lockup: Seal + "The AI Banking Institute" (Cormorant SC, 15px, 1.5px letter-spacing) with "aibankinginstitute.com" below in DM Mono 8px. This is the nav lockup.

Secondary lockup: Seal alone (for favicon, app icon, social avatar).

Tertiary lockup: Full name stacked without seal (for documents where the seal would be too small to read).

Minimum clear space: The width of the letter "A" in the seal on all sides. Never crowd the mark.
What the Mark Should NOT Look Like
Not a tech company icon. Not a bank logo with columns or eagles. Not a shield. Not a hexagon. The circle with fine ruling is the direction — it reads as institutional, verified, and slightly ancient in the best way. Think less "fintech app icon" and more "embossed seal on a legal document." The AiBI mark should look appropriate on both a digital screen and a certificate of achievement.

Components from

Buttons
Primary (btn-terra): Solid terra bg, linen text, 13px 28px padding, 2px radius, no border

Secondary (btn-outline): Transparent bg, border2 color border, ink3 text — hover to terra border + terra text

CTA reversed (btn-cream): Linen bg, terra text — used on terra backgrounds only

Ghost reversed (btn-lo): Transparent, 35% white border, 80% white text — used on cobalt/dark backgrounds

All buttons: uppercase, 11px, DM Sans 600, letter-spacing 1.2px, 2px border-radius
Cards
Standard card: Parch2 bg, terra border 1px, 3px radius. Top accent: 2px gradient from terra → transparent (leftward). Label in Cormorant SC 9px terra, 2.5px letter-spacing. Title in Cormorant 20px 600. Body in DM Sans 13px slate.

Pillar cards: Parch bg, large background number (opacity .05). Top accent 3px in pillar color. Letter label in Cormorant SC 13px, 3px letter-spacing.

Certification cards: Parch bg, border2 border. Bottom accent 2px terra (revealed on hover via scaleX transform). Hover state: translateY(-3px), box shadow terra.

Engagement cards: Linen bg, no accent line. Hover: background darkens to parch.
Form Elements
Text input: Parch2 bg, border2 border, 2px radius, 10px 12px padding, DM Mono 17px. Focus: terra border.

Range slider: Terra accent-color. Default browser with terra color override.

Result display box: Parch3 bg, terra border. Display number in Cormorant 52px 700 terra. Range/subtitle in DM Sans 11px dust.

Layout Rules
Max content width: 1200px, centered with 60px horizontal padding (24px on mobile).
Section padding: 88px top and bottom on desktop, 64px on mobile.
Grid: 12-column. Primary layouts use 2-col (1fr 1fr), 3-col (repeat(3,1fr)), and asymmetric (1fr + fixed sidebar).
Gap: 24px standard gap between cards. 1px gap between grid-joined components (like pillar blocks).
Border radius: 2–4px throughout. Never more than 4px on structural elements. Circles are for marks and icons only.
Breakpoint: Mobile at 1000px. The design is desktop-first; mobile is a graceful degradation.
