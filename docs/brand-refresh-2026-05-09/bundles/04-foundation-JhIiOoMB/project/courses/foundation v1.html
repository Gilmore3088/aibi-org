▸ Free preview Module 07 · L2 The four-pillar prompt Reading 4 of 7

Every prompt worth saving has four parts.

A reading · about three minutes

Reusable prompts are the difference between a one-off chat and a working tool. The bankers who pull ahead this year are the ones who write a prompt once and use it every week — not the ones who keep typing variations of the same request into a fresh chat.

Every prompt worth saving has four parts: a Role for the AI, the Inputs you'll paste, the Constraints (what to avoid), and the Output you want back. Skip any one of them and the prompt becomes a chat — useful once, and forgotten by Wednesday.

"Member-services rep needs to rewrite a rate-change letter. The chat version says 'rewrite this nicer.' The tool version names the role, names the constraint (no implied promises), and names the output (one paragraph, plain language)." — Module 07, Lesson 2

§ 03
How a module flows

Read it, see it, do it, get it reviewed.

Every one of the twelve modules has the same shape. No surprise quizzes. No video-watch theatre. No certificate for showing up — only for finishing the work.

Step one i

Read

A short reading — three to five sections, plain language, written for adults who already know banking. Read like an essay, not a textbook.

~8 minutes
Step two ii

See

One real-world example, drawn from a community bank or credit union. The before-and-after, the trade-off, the call you'd actually make.

~6 minutes
Step three iii

Do

An exercise on a real task you do every week. Built in our Playground — your work, your data (or our sample), your saved artifact.

~12 minutes
Step four iv

Reviewed

A human reviewer reads what you produced and writes you back — what to keep, what to redo, why. No auto-grader. No 'B+ for trying.'

~3 days return

Common questions

Asked & answered.

More on the full FAQ page. The seven most common are below.

Q · 01 How long will this actually take me? +

About six hours of reading and exercises across the twelve modules. The reviewed feedback adds a day or two between modules — but that's calendar time, not your time.

Most learners finish in three to six weeks doing one or two modules a week at lunch. You have ninety days from enrolment, but no time pressure inside that window.

Q · 02 What if my bank's policy says no AI tools? +

You'll do fine. The Playground inside the course is sandboxed; nothing you paste leaves the Institute, and we never train models on your work. Module 03 (the privacy line) is built specifically to help you stay inside any reasonable policy.

Several charter-cohort learners completed Foundations on banks with restrictive policies, and used the artifacts to write the policy that came next.

Q · 03 Is this technical? I'm not a programmer. +

No code. No math. The most technical it gets is Module 04, where we explain how a language model predicts — using analogies, not equations. If you can write a memo, you can do this course.

Q · 04 What's the credential worth? +

The AiBI-Foundations credential is a verifiable digital badge that says you've shipped reviewed work — not watched videos. We're working with several state banking associations and the NCUA to recognise it for continuing-education credit; your local mileage may vary.

Honestly, the artifacts are worth more than the badge. Frame the badge if you like; use the artifacts on Monday.
