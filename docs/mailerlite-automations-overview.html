The big picture

Every banker who completes the AI readiness assessment, and every visitor who signs up to the AI Banking Brief, lands in MailerLite. From there, one of five automations fires automatically, based on which group the subscriber joined.

The five automations are:

Automation Trigger Group Emails Subscriber Outcome
AiBI Newsletter — Welcome AI Banking Brief Newsletter 1 One welcome email confirming subscription, setting expectation of one issue every two weeks.
AiBI Assessment — Starting Point Tier · Starting Point 3 (Day 0, 3, 7) Banker scored 12–20. Sequence orients them, gives one cheap pilot, and ends with the funding-conversation email.
AiBI Assessment — Early Stage Tier · Early Stage 3 (Day 0, 3, 7) Banker scored 21–29. Sequence pushes convergence on one tool, a one-page governance memo, and a 90-day arc.
AiBI Assessment — Building Momentum Tier · Building Momentum 3 (Day 0, 3, 7) Banker scored 30–38. Sequence frames the capability ladder and the habits of the next maturity band.
AiBI Assessment — Ready to Scale Tier · Ready to Scale 3 (Day 0, 3, 7) Banker scored 39–48. Sequence positions them as peer leaders and surfaces the institution-wide credential program plus Leadership Advisory.

"Free assessment in. Right tier sequence out. Three emails in seven days. The right CTA at the right tier."

How it is wired

The data flow, end to end

  1. Banker takes the free assessment at /assessment/start. Twelve questions, scored on a 12–48 scale.
  2. The result page shows their tier (Starting Point, Early Stage, Building Momentum, or Ready to Scale).
  3. Banker enters their email for the dimension breakdown plus a starter artifact tied to their lowest-scoring dimension.
  4. The browser POSTs to /api/capture-email with the score, tier, and (when consent is given) marketingOptIn: true.
  5. The route writes to Supabase (user_profiles table) and synchronously calls tagAssessmentTier() in src/lib/mailerlite/sequences.ts.
  6. MailerLite upserts the subscriber via POST /api/subscribers, attaching them to two groups: AI Readiness Assessment (the master list) and the matching tier group.
  7. MailerLite sees the new group membership and fires the matching automation. The Day 0 email is queued immediately.
  8. Days 3 and 7 fire on schedule from MailerLite's clock, with no further server interaction.

Why marketing opt-in is required

The route deliberately does not subscribe a banker to MailerLite when they have not opted in. They still receive their assessment results (transactional, via Resend), but the nurture sequence is consent-gated. This honors CAN-SPAM and matches the language used on the consent checkbox.

Voice and content rules

Every email follows the same content rules, derived from the brand brief and the design system. These are non-negotiable across all 13 emails.

What every email does

  • Uses the full name The AI Banking Institute in prose. AiBI appears only in credential codes (AiBI-Practitioner, AiBI-S, AiBI-L) and the wordmark.
  • Cites every statistic by source, year, and publication. No unsourced claims.
  • Sets numbers in DM Mono so they read with weight. Percentages stay as 55% (brand rule overrides Every's "55 percent").
  • Uses italics, never bold, for emphasis in prose. Bold tags are reserved for visual labels (table cells, list lead-ins).
  • Em dashes carry no surrounding spaces. X—Y, never X — Y.
  • Subject lines act as the headline (title case). The H1 inside the body acts as a subheading (sentence case, no terminal period).
  • No semicolons in email copy. (Hard Every-style rule, accepted.)
  • No emoji, no gradients, no "AI-powered" buzzwords.

What every CTA looks like

  • Day 0 emails point to the full results page on the site.
  • Mid-sequence (Day 3) emails point to the Executive Briefing (free, 30 minutes) or the Practitioner credential.
  • End-of-sequence (Day 7) emails point to the credential program or Leadership Advisory, scaled to the band.

Where the canonical copy lives

The version-controlled source of truth is src/lib/mailerlite/email-content.ts. The MailerLite dashboard editor is downstream. If an operator polishes typography in the dashboard, that change does not propagate back to the repo — to keep both in sync, edit the file first and recreate the automations.

How to activate (the manual steps)

The five automations are created via API, but MailerLite requires each email step to be opened in the visual editor and saved at least once before the automation can flip from Draft to Active. This is a one-time setup pass.

  1. Open the automation list at dashboard.mailerlite.com/automations. Click the Drafts tab.
  2. Click "Edit" on one automation (recommend starting with the Newsletter — it has only one email step).
  3. Click on the first email block in the workflow editor. The right-hand panel shows From name, From email, Subject, and a "Design email" button.
  4. Set the From fields if not already filled: From name James Gilmore, From email hello@aibankinginstitute.com (the verified sender).
  5. Click "Design email". The visual editor loads the HTML I generated. Click "Done editing" or "Save and exit" — no changes required.
  6. Repeat for each email step in that automation. Tier sequences have three; the Newsletter has one.
  7. Click "Activate" at the top of the workflow. The automation moves from Drafts to Workflows.
  8. Repeat for the remaining four automations.

Day-to-day operations

How to edit copy

Two ways. The dashboard route is fastest for typo fixes; the repo route is the source of truth.

  • Dashboard edit (fast): Open the automation, open the email step, edit in the visual editor, save. Effective immediately for new subscribers; subscribers already mid-sequence receive what was queued at the time they joined.
  • Repo edit (canonical): Edit src/lib/mailerlite/email-content.ts, commit, then in the dashboard either edit the email step manually OR delete and recreate the automation via the API.

How to add a new tier email

The current cadence is Day 0, 3, 7. To add a Day 14 follow-up:

  1. Add the new email to email-content.ts as a fourth entry in the tier's EMAILS array.
  2. Add 14 to the tier's delaysDays array.
  3. Either delete and recreate the automation via the MailerLite MCP, or open the automation in the dashboard and add a new email step manually after the Day 7 step.

How to retake handling works

If a banker takes the assessment again and lands in a different tier, the route detects the change (priorTierId !== newTier) and removes them from the old tier group before adding them to the new one. The old tier's automation stops queuing further emails for them. The new tier's automation fires from Day 0 again.

What is NOT in MailerLite

  • The Resend transactional emails (results breakdown, course purchase, certificate, inquiry ack). Those run through five Resend Templates, separate from MailerLite. See the 2026-05-06 entry in CLAUDE.md.
  • Supabase Auth emails (signup confirm, password reset, magic link). Those run through Supabase Custom SMTP using Resend as transport.
  • The recurring AI Banking Brief newsletter issues. Those will be Campaigns in MailerLite, sent on your schedule. The automation here is only the welcome.
