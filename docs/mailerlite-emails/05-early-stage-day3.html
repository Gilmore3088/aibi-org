Quick follow-up.

You do not need a 40-page AI policy. You need a one-page memo that your board can read, your compliance officer can defend, and your front-line bankers will remember.

The shape worth keeping in mind:

What is allowed

Sanctioned tool or tools, with named data classifications: “public information only”, “internal non-PII”, “do not paste customer PII into any AI tool, ever.”

Who owns oversight

One named person — usually the CIO or CRO — is the AI accountable executive. Not a committee.

How exceptions get approved

Single-page request form, one approver, five-day SLA. No committees. No quarterly reviews.

What gets logged

Every use case enters the use-case inventory before going live. The AIEOG AI Lexicon's “AI use case inventory” expectation maps to this.

The relevant regulatory anchors are SR 11-7 (model risk management), the Interagency TPRM Guidance (third-party risk), ECOA/Reg B (fair lending if any AI touches credit), and the AIEOG AI Lexicon (US Treasury, FBIIC, and FSSCC, February 2026).
