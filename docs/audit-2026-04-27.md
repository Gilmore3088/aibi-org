**Branch:** `codex/4.24-update` · **Scope:** entire site + 4 worktrees · **Method:** parallel source-read (Phase 1) + live dev-server smoke test (Phase 2, :3001) · **Lens:** McKinsey × Coursera, built for community bankers, direct.

## 0. Anti-Pattern Verdict — PASS WITH CAVEATS

The site **does not look AI-generated.** No gradients, no glassmorphism, no rounded blobs, no emoji, no hero metrics, no stock photos, no "AI-powered" badges. Color tokens are honored on the homepage (zero hardcoded hex). Typography hierarchy (Cormorant display + Cormorant SC eyebrows + DM Sans body + DM Mono numerals with `tabular-nums`) is exactly the McKinsey/Economist register the brief calls for.

**Tells that still feel SaaS-ish:**
- The typing-dot loader and per-line fade in `InteractiveSkillsPreview.tsx:226–240` is the one moment that reads "AI demo" instead of "consulting deliverable."
- 12+ instances of `font-sans … italic` site-wide (banned per brand rules — italics belong to Cormorant only).
- Pillar color leaks in 6+ components (sage and cobalt used as decorative accents instead of strict pillar markers).

The site clears the AI-slop bar. The remaining gap is **brand discipline**, not aesthetic taste.

## 2. Golden Flow Critique — Landing → Assessment → AiBI Foundations → Modules

### Landing (`/`)
- Hero presents two equal CTAs (`/assessment/start` + `/courses/aibi-p`). The closing band repeats the pattern. **4 homepage CTAs split 2/2 across two destinations is a fork, not a funnel.**
- `ROICalculator` mid-scroll ships warm traffic to **Calendly**, a third destination. For a self-serve learner-first product this is wrong.
- "Trust strip" claims are unsourced ("Aligned with banking expectations") even though the Footer already has the correct citation string ("Aligned with SR 11-7, Interagency TPRM Guidance, ECOA/Reg B, AIEOG").
- 11 of the 13 section components in `src/components/sections/` are unused — `HeroRotating`, `ChooseYourPath`, `WideningGap`, `ThreeFears`, `FinalCTABand`, `MonthlyCadence`, `SecurityBand`, `TransformationScoreboard`, `CertificationCards`, `SampleQuestion`, `InstitutionsTeaser`. Maintenance debt + invites drift.

### Assessment (`/assessment/start` → `/assessment` → email gate → results)
- **The code does the opposite of what CLAUDE.md says.** `page.tsx:59` reads "Score and tier visible immediately — no email gate" while CLAUDE.md decision log (2026-04-15) says score is now gated. **Resolve this contradiction before launch — it's the single biggest funnel question.**
- **Tap-to-advance with no Next button and no Back navigation.** `QuestionCard.tsx` already accepts `onBack`/`canGoBack` props but `page.tsx:48–54` never wires them. On a 12-question diagnostic this will produce abandonment and bad data.
- v2 question pool is materially better than v1 (real regulatory anchors: SR 11-7, TPRM, ECOA/Reg B, BSA/AML). v1 is still imported and shipped — pick one.
- Two leading/unanswerable items: `bp-06` ("would staff be willing to spend 30–60 minutes…" — obvious answer) and `cau-06` ("roughly what % of staff have used AI in past 30 days?" — bankers cannot answer this honestly in 3 min).
- **Calendly fallback is `CALENDLY_URL ?? '#'`** (`page.tsx:12`). If env var is unset in prod, the briefing CTA is silently `href="#"` — invisible conversion leak.
- Two competing primary CTAs in 30 seconds: score screen says "Explore the AiBI Foundations"; ResultsView says "Start Your First AI Practice Rep". Pick one.
- Score-tier copy in `ResultsViewV2.tsx:238–330` is the **strongest copy on the entire site** — concrete failure modes and product routing. Lift this voice to the homepage.

### AiBI Foundations (`/courses/aibi-p`)
- **Onboarding is a survey, not a value demo.** A banker doesn't write a single prompt or see one AI output before being routed to Module 1. No "first AI win in 90 seconds" hook.
- `[module]/page.tsx:73-76` overrides the rich legacy `module-N.ts` content with `V4_AIBIP_MODULES`, where every module is **two short paragraphs + one tryThis line + one free-text textarea**. The legacy `module-1.ts` (193 lines, sourced regulatory tables, multi-section narrative) is **no longer rendered.**
- The Apply tab is **identical across all 12 modules** — paste-text-and-reflect. That's a wiki entry, not a class.
- **None of the 12 modules generates a real downloadable artifact** (.md / PDF / checklist) at completion, despite the user's explicit requirement: "1–3 immediate actions per module they can experience in one class — a skill, .md, agent, idea — that helps them in their bank job today."
- A learner can "complete" the course in **12 minutes by pasting 20 characters into 12 textareas** — completion gate is `value.length >= 20` only.
- Sandbox is real and well-built (Anthropic streaming, PII scanner, downloadable conversation), but **stops at module 9.** M10–M12 (the most role-applied modules) have no Practice tab.
- `ClassificationDrill.tsx` exists but is unused for M9 (red/yellow/green classifier — the perfect Apply for the safe-AI-use module).
- Toolkit references modules (1/4/5/6) that don't match V4's 12-module schema — IA mismatch.
- **Sidebar omits Prompt Library + Tool Guides** — two of the most reusable banker assets are orphaned (only reachable by URL).
- ChatGPT/Gemini sandbox tabs are stubs (`AIPracticeSandbox.tsx:13-17`); the data file `tool-guides-copilot-gemini.ts` is never imported.

### Module Pages (`/courses/aibi-p/[module]`)
- See course findings above. Per-module summary in the dispatched audit — M1 is the strongest, M11 promises a "personal prompt library" but Apply is still the same generic textarea, M12 is just "submit final lab." Practice ends at M9.

## 4. Patterns & Systemic Issues

1. **Scaffolding-rich, content-poor.** The sandbox, certificate path, progress tracking, role-aware filtering, RLS policies are all genuinely good. The content beneath them is shallow.
2. **Brand grammar drifts at the component layer.** Homepage is disciplined; deeper pages (courses, AiBI-S/L, verify, journey banner) keep using sage/cobalt as a decorative palette and italicize DM Sans.
3. **CTAs split intent at every layer.** Hero (2), closing band (2), ROI calculator (1 to Calendly), score screen (1), results screen (1 different). At each step the user can be pulled three directions.
4. **"Quick Win Sprint" / "fCAIO" residue.** Despite both retirements, the strings live on in shipping content (exam, PDFs, AiBI-L sessions, layout metadata).
5. **Apply work is text-input where it should be artifact-output.** Every banker who completes a module should walk away with a file they can put on their desktop. Today they walk away with a textarea.
