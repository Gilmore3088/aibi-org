## Pillar 1 — Visual Hierarchy & Composition

**Score: 3/4**

**Summary:** The post-distillation homepage is genuinely good — one CTA per
section, four equal-weight steps in the How It Works grid, a single
closing terra band. ResultsViewV2 paces beautifully via `space-y-16`. The
two surfaces still pulling the score down are AiBI-P course root (too many
small chips competing with the hero) and AiBI-L (4xl–7xl headings over
muted dust subhead, which reads as marketing dazzle rather than executive
restraint).

**Findings:**

- **Header has two competing primary CTAs.** `src/components/Header.tsx:31-45`
  ships both an "Assessment" nav link and a filled terra "Take Assessment"
  button. On mobile the whole right side becomes one terra-shouting cluster.
  Recommendation: drop the "Assessment" nav item and keep the button, or
  remove the button on routes that already start with the assessment CTA
  (homepage, /assessment).
- **AiBI-P course root hero is busy.** `src/app/courses/aibi-p/page.tsx:90-125` —
  H1 + 3-line description + one filled CTA + four mono chips ("12 modules",
  "Learn / Practice / Apply", "120 min total", optional "X/12 complete") all
  on one row. Three chips would land better than four. The "Learn / Practice
  / Apply" chip is a process explanation, not a metric — it deserves its own
  band, not a chip slot.
- **Closing terra band on `/` is excellent.** `src/app/page.tsx:125-146` —
  exactly the right composition: one eyebrow, one large headline, one body
  paragraph, one button. Preserve this pattern as the canonical "section
  closer".
- **AiBI-L hero leans dramatic.** `src/app/courses/aibi-l/page.tsx:57-67` uses
  `text-5xl sm:text-6xl lg:text-7xl font-bold` for the H1 and an italic dust
  subhead. The size is justifiable on a standalone product page, but the
  subhead is `text-[color:var(--color-dust)]` at 1.25rem-2xl which is below
  AA on linen for the smaller breakpoint.
- **MaturityScorecard pacing.** `src/app/courses/aibi-l/_components/MaturityScorecard.tsx:102, 266`
  uses italic dust for explanatory copy in two places — same dust
  readability concern, plus DM-Sans-italic risk (see Pillar 2).
- **ResultsViewV2 is the best-composed surface in the app.** Score → tier
  headline → tier interpretation card → top-3 gaps → starter artifact →
  recommended next step → first practice → dimension grid → newsletter →
  print. Eight sections, one job each, generous `space-y-16`. This is the
  reference composition.

## Pillar 2 — Typography Discipline

**Score: 2/4**

**Summary:** The brand rule "never italicize DM Sans" is documented in
CLAUDE.md as non-negotiable. There are 20+ places where `italic` is applied
to elements that resolve to DM Sans (no `font-serif` ancestor in scope, or
`font-mono`). This is the single biggest typographic gap. Cormorant SC,
DM Mono, and tabular-nums usage on numbers is otherwise excellent.

**Findings:**

- **DM-Sans-italic violations** — every instance where `italic` is on body
  copy without `font-serif`:
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-p/onboarding/SurveyBranding.tsx:44, 121`
    (`className="italic"` and `className="text-base italic leading-relaxed"`)
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-p/_components/AcceptableUseCardForm.tsx:66, 70`,
    `ActivityForm.tsx:74, 104`, `IterationFields.tsx:60, 73`,
    `SkillBuilder Panels.tsx:279`, `SkillDiagnosis.tsx:211, 220` — all the
    "No response" empty-state spans use raw `italic` (DM Sans body) — should
    be `font-serif italic` or `font-mono` "—" placeholder
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-p/_components/RolePathCard.tsx:253` (text-xs italic)
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-p/certificate/page.tsx:98, 113` (`text-sm italic`
    on banker-facing certificate description)
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-s/page.tsx:32` (track placeholder italic on
    `font-mono` parent — italicizing DM Mono)
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-s/ops/unit/[unitId]/_components/{RubricScore,UnitRenderer,ApplyBeat,DefendBeat,PersonaChat}.tsx`
    — 5 separate `text-sm italic` / `italic text-sm` on DM Sans copy
  - `src/app/resources/the-skill-not-the-prompt/page.tsx:159`,
    `ai-governance-without-the-jargon/page.tsx:266` — `text-sm
    text-[color:var(--color-ink)]/70 italic` on body paragraphs
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-l/page.tsx:142, 241`,
    `src/app/courses/aibi-s/purchase/page.tsx:227, 253` — `<span
    className="italic">` inside H1/H2 elements that DO carry `font-serif` on
    parent → these are OK (Cormorant italic) and a nice editorial flourish.
    Do not change. Distinguish "italic-on-serif-heading" (legitimate) from
    "italic-on-sans-body" (violation).
- **Recommendation:** add a one-time codemod that converts every bare
  `italic` to `font-serif italic` (and review the AiBI-S/Ops unit beats —
  those are short status strings where italic isn't needed at all; just drop
  it).
- **Cormorant + font-bold combination** is heavy in 86 places across 45 files.
  Cormorant Garamond Bold (700) is loaded, so this works, but the
  designer-brief discipline favors Cormorant 600 (semibold) for editorial
  weight. Consider sweeping `font-serif font-bold` → `font-serif font-semibold`
  in headings under 4xl.
- **Excellent number discipline.** `font-mono tabular-nums` shows up
  consistently in ResultsViewV2 (lines 110, 193, 261), QuestionCard top
  meta (line 67), and ROI inputs. The 1.25 line-height on `font-serif
  text-3xl md:text-4xl leading-tight` headings is a reliable signature.
- **Eyebrow labels.** `font-serif-sc text-xs uppercase tracking-[0.2em]
  text-[color:var(--color-terra)]` is used as a section eyebrow ~50 times —
  consistent and on-brand. Tracking values vary (0.18em, 0.2em, 0.22em);
  pick one and ship a `.eyebrow` utility class.
- **Mixed eyebrow/label fonts.** Some eyebrows are `font-mono text-xs
  uppercase tracking-widest` (e.g. `QuestionCard.tsx:67-72`,
  `assessment/page.tsx:73`, `ResultsViewV2 PrintButton`) and others are
  `font-serif-sc tracking-[0.2em]`. Both look good; pick a convention
  ("mono for numbered/system labels, serif-sc for editorial eyebrows" is
  defensible) and document it in CLAUDE.md.

## Pillar 3 — Color & Pillar Discipline

**Score: 3/4**

**Summary:** Pillar discipline is genuinely clean. AiBI-L is now cobalt
end-to-end, AiBI-P is terra end-to-end, AiBI-S correctly carries cobalt
for security/ops contexts. The only user-facing red/green Tailwind leak is
inside `/admin/reviewer` which is back-office and exempt. The dust token
is being used as body copy in 23 files, which crosses the WCAG-AA line on
`text-base` paragraphs — the token's own globals.css comment says "large
text only (2.8:1)".

**Findings:**

- **No user-facing Tailwind red/green leaks.** The only matches for
  `bg-red-50` / `text-red-700` are in `src/app/admin/reviewer/page.tsx:58`
  (back-office reviewer queue, unauth-gated). Acceptable.
- **Verify page is brand-clean.** `src/app/verify/[certificateId]/page.tsx`
  uses inline-styled CSS variables exclusively — terra checkmark on success,
  error border on the not-found badge. No Tailwind token leakage. This file
  is the right reference for "high-stakes single-purpose page".
- **Dust on body copy is the live AA risk.** `text-[color:var(--color-dust)]`
  appears in 23 files including:
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-l/page.tsx:65` (`text-xl sm:text-2xl` italic
    subhead — large enough at 2xl, borderline at xl)
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-l/[session]/page.tsx:116` (`text-lg italic
    leading-relaxed` body paragraph — fails AA on linen)
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-l/request/page.tsx:90, 128` — same
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-s/purchase/page.tsx:108` — `text-lg` body lede
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-l/_components/MaturityScorecard.tsx:102, 266` —
    `text-sm` explanatory copy → fails AA outright
  - `src/lib/course-harness/CourseSidebar.tsx`, `CourseMobileDrawer.tsx`,
    `src/app/courses/aibi-p/_components/CourseSidebar.tsx`,
    `MobileSidebarDrawer.tsx` — sidebar metadata at small sizes (failing AA
    if the dust is on linen background)
  - `src/app/courses/aibi-p/_components/SkillBuilder.tsx`,
    `IterationTracker.tsx`, `SubscriptionInventory.tsx` etc. — same pattern
- **Recommendation:** add a token `--color-slate-soft` (or just use
  `text-[color:var(--color-slate)]/85`) for the "secondary copy" job, and
  reserve dust for ≥18px non-essential metadata. A find-and-replace of
  `text-[color:var(--color-dust)]` on anything `text-sm`/`text-base` is the
  one-shot fix.
- **Light-tint token usage is consistent.** terra-pale appears only as
  `bg-[color:var(--color-terra-pale)]/40` for selected radio options
  (`QuestionCard.tsx:111`) and matched hover (`/20`). Sage-pale and
  cobalt-pale are not over-used.
- **Plain `text-[color:var(--color-ink)]/50` and `/60` labels** appear in
  `Header.tsx:35`, `assessment/page.tsx:120-129` etc. Ink at 50% opacity is
  ~3:1 on linen at 12-14px — that's UI, not body, so it passes AA-large/AA-UI
  but is at the floor. Bumping to /70 would buy headroom.
- **No hardcoded hex anywhere user-facing.** PDF generators
  (`CertificateDocument.tsx`, `AcceptableUseCardDocument.tsx`,
  `SafeAIUseGuideDocument.tsx`) carry hex per the CLAUDE.md exemption.
  Acceptable.

## Pillar 4 — Copy & Voice

**Score: 3/4**

**Summary:** The voice is exactly right where it has been intentionally
authored: ResultsViewV2 tier interpretations (lines 257-348) name concrete
failure modes ("the teller who saves 90 minutes a week never tells the
operations team"), the starter artifacts (content/assessments/v2/starter-artifacts.ts)
are written like internal memos, the home How It Works is one verb +
one sentence per step. Where it slips is in places that haven't been
re-touched since the distillation — AiBI-L marketing copy, some tooling
descriptions.

**Findings:**

- **Hero is tight.** `src/app/page.tsx:51-76` — "Teach your team how to use
  AI safely at work." → "Turning bankers into builders." → "In three
  minutes, see your readiness level, your top gaps, and the first practical
  exercise to complete." → CTA. Three sentences, no filler, no
  "transformative". Preserve.
- **Tier interpretations are the voice's high-water mark.**
  `src/app/assessment/_components/ResultsViewV2.tsx:257-348` — every tier
  paragraph names a concrete operational fact ("AI use case inventory",
  "documented before-and-after impact"), an honest framing ("This is not a
  problem. It is a starting position."), and a specific recommendation.
  This is the McKinsey × Coursera bar.
- **Starter artifacts are equally strong.**
  `content/assessments/v2/starter-artifacts.ts` — three actions, one
  starter prompt, one "why this is the right first step", citations strip.
  No "AI-powered", no "transformative". Sourced to AIEOG AI Lexicon and
  SR 11-7 by name. Preserve as the content-template-of-record.
- **AiBI-L copy is dressier.** `src/app/courses/aibi-l/page.tsx:65-68` ("a
  board-ready AI presentation built with your numbers" is good, but the
  italic dust treatment fights the directness).
- **InteractiveSkillsPreview output samples** (`InteractiveSkillsPreview.tsx`)
  are excellent — they show, not tell, what AI output looks like in a
  banking context. Keep this section as a homepage anchor.
- **Trust strip.** `page.tsx:30-35` — four bullets, four words each, no
  filler. ("No PII required", "Human review required", "Designed for real
  workflows, not experiments", "Aligned with banking expectations".)
  Preserve.
- **Live site doesn't yet display the named statistics.** CLAUDE.md tracks
  9 sourced stats (Bank Director 2024, FDIC Q4 2024, Personetics 2025,
  etc.) and explicitly forbids unsourced statistics. The home page
  doesn't show *any* of them. That's not a violation, but it's a missed
  proof-point opportunity — a small "Why now" band with three sourced
  stats + named citations would significantly raise institutional
  authority without raising buzzword count.
- **No "FFIEC-aware" anywhere in src.** Verified clean.
- **Mono-cased eyebrows on `/` ("ROI Model", "Trust", "How it works",
  "Ready when you are")** are direct and on-brand. Prefer one-or-two-word
  eyebrows over phrase-eyebrows site-wide.

## Pillar 5 — Interaction & Affordance

**Score: 3/4**

**Summary:** The assessment is genuinely class-leading on interaction.
Roving tabindex, arrow-key + Home/End nav, focus-on-question-change,
focus-on-score-reveal, error-on-blur reset, sessionStorage-aware
pre-hydration skeleton, Calendly URL guarded by a real route fallback,
disabled state on submit, server-side persistence of dimension breakdown.
Two surfaces drag the score down: Header CTA collision and the
`StarterArtifactCard` clipboard-fail path.

**Findings:**

- **QuestionCard keyboard model is excellent.**
  `src/app/assessment/_components/QuestionCard.tsx:40-63` implements
  arrow-up/down/left/right, Home, End with wrap-around, plus `tabindex`
  rotation. `aria-checked` and `aria-label` (with "(selected)" suffix)
  are set correctly. Reference implementation.
- **Score-reveal focus management.** `assessment/page.tsx:30-39, 83-87` —
  on completion, focus jumps to the score heading via
  `requestAnimationFrame` with `tabIndex={-1}` and `focus:outline-none` on
  the heading itself. Screen-reader announcement + keyboard-user safety —
  both nailed.
- **Pre-hydration skeleton.** `assessment/page.tsx:183-207` — exactly the
  right shape (label row, heading, four option rows). Prevents the FOIT
  jump on slow phones.
- **EmailGate forgiveness.** `EmailGate.tsx:108-115` — typing into the
  field clears the error state and message. ARIA `aria-describedby` and
  `aria-invalid` correctly wired.
- **Header CTA collision.** `src/components/Header.tsx:31-45` — same
  destination (`/assessment/start`) appears twice in the right rail (text
  link + filled button). On a 1280px viewport this is two terra clusters
  next to each other. Either drop the "Assessment" nav link or hide the
  button on routes that already promote the CTA.
- **StarterArtifactCard fallback ambiguity.**
  `StarterArtifactCard.tsx:25-34` — clipboard write failure is silently
  swallowed with the comment "the download button remains available as a
  fallback", but no UI cue tells the banker the copy didn't happen. On
  iOS Safari in a non-secure context (which is how some banks browse) the
  Copy button will appear to do nothing. Flip the catch to `setCopied`
  → "Copy failed — use Download" and clear after 2s.
- **Calendly URL guard is the right pattern.** `assessment/page.tsx:14-15`:
  `BRIEFING_URL = process.env.NEXT_PUBLIC_CALENDLY_URL ?? '/for-institutions/advisory'`.
  Comment explicitly says "Never use '#'". Reference pattern for any
  external-link fallback.
- **"Start over" is honest.** `assessment/page.tsx:155-161` — small mono
  link, not a dramatic destructive button. Right register.
- **CTAs name what they do.** "Take the Assessment", "Show my full
  results", "Start Your First AI Practice Rep", "Book Your Executive
  Briefing", "Copy to clipboard", "Download .md". No "Submit", no "Click
  here", no "Get started" abstraction. This is a clean win.
- **Back button is wired and visible only when applicable.**
  `QuestionCard.tsx:124-134` — invisible placeholder maintains layout when
  `canGoBack` is false. Prevents the layout pop.

## Pillar 6 — Accessibility & Resilience

**Score: 3/4**

**Summary:** The accessibility scaffolding is unusually good for a startup
codebase: skip-link, focus-visible outline, prefers-reduced-motion,
ARIA radiogroup with proper roving tabindex, sr-only utility, focus
management on phase transitions. The two open issues are dust-on-body-copy
contrast (covered in Pillar 3) and a few `text-ink/50` UI labels that sit
at the AA-UI floor.

**Findings:**

- **Global a11y plumbing.** `src/app/globals.css:43-78` — `*:focus-visible`
  ring, `.sr-only`, `.skip-link` with reveal-on-focus animation,
  `prefers-reduced-motion` media query that nukes animation/transition
  durations. `src/app/layout.tsx:124-133` renders the skip link and
  `#main-content` target. Reference implementation.
- **ARIA radiogroup.** `QuestionCard.tsx:82` — `role="radiogroup"` with
  `aria-label={question.prompt}` on the wrapper, `role="radio"` +
  `aria-checked` + `aria-label` on each option. Correct.
- **Focus-on-mount for question prompt.** `QuestionCard.tsx:33-35` — the
  H2 prompt is `tabIndex={-1}` and receives focus on `question.id` change,
  so screen readers announce the new question.
- **Pre-hydration skeleton is `aria-hidden`.**
  `assessment/page.tsx:185` — correct, since it carries no real content.
- **Print stylesheet is sophisticated.** `globals.css:108-156` —
  `data-print-hide="true"` selectors hide chrome,
  `.print-avoid-break` prevents awkward page breaks on score ring and
  dimension grid, a print-only footer carries the brand line and email.
  ResultsViewV2 uses these consistently (lines 61, 71, 88, 96, 167, 177,
  217, 222, 230). Genuinely thoughtful.
- **Newsletter and Print buttons have the right semantics.** Neither
  ships as an `<a href="#">`. Print is a `<button>`, Newsletter is a
  `<form>` with proper `<label>`.
- **Mobile resilience.** `assessment/page.tsx:53` is `min-h-screen`,
  hero sections use `px-6` not pixel widths, no horizontal-scroll candidates
  spotted in the audited surfaces. ROI calculator collapses to a single
  column on mobile.
- **Long banker name in dimension grid.** `ResultsViewV2.tsx:188-211` —
  `flex items-baseline justify-between` with mono score on the right.
  Truly long dimension labels would wrap; the design holds. Tested
  conceptually, not in viewport.
- **Open: dust contrast.** Repeated from Pillar 3. The single highest-impact
  a11y fix is to change body-copy `text-[color:var(--color-dust)]` to
  `text-[color:var(--color-slate)]` on every element where the type is
  `text-base` or `text-sm`.
- **Open: ink/50 metadata labels.** `Header.tsx:35`,
  `assessment/page.tsx:122, 129` etc. — at 12px these labels are at the
  AA-UI floor on linen. Bumping to /65 or /70 keeps the visual register
  but buys contrast headroom.
- **No `lang` attribute on inline foreign-language strings.** Not a
  current issue but flag for future copy that quotes non-English regulatory
  text.

## Cross-cutting observations

- **There are two parallel "Course Sidebar" implementations**:
  `src/lib/course-harness/CourseSidebar.tsx` and
  `src/app/courses/aibi-p/_components/CourseSidebar.tsx`. Both reach
  for `text-[color:var(--color-dust)]`. Consolidate to one.
- **Eyebrow tracking values vary**: 0.18em, 0.2em, 0.22em, sometimes
  `tracking-widest` (~0.1em). The three values are within design tolerance
  but they're not the same — pick one tracking step per eyebrow font
  (serif-sc vs mono) and ship `.eyebrow-serif` and `.eyebrow-mono` utility
  classes.
- **Cormorant 700 ("font-bold") is used heavily** (86 occurrences across
  45 files). For editorial restraint, `font-semibold` (600) on H1/H2 under
  4xl reads more like the McKinsey-Garamond tradition; reserve 700 for
  display-scale (5xl+) hero heads.
- **The starter artifact + tier interpretation pair** is the strongest
  content artifact in the repo. Use it as the prose-reference for the next
  copy pass on AiBI-L marketing pages, the For-Institutions advisory page,
  and the resources articles.

## Top 5 fixes ranked by impact

1. **Italic-on-DM-Sans codemod.** Brand-rule violation in 20+ surfaces.
   Sweep every bare `italic` to either `font-serif italic` (when stylized
   prose is intended) or drop the italic entirely (status strings like
   "Grading your defense…" don't need it). Highest-leverage fix —
   single PR, low risk, restores a non-negotiable brand rule.
2. **Audit `text-[color:var(--color-dust)]` on body copy.** Cross-reference
   23 files for any usage at `text-base` or smaller; change to
   `text-[color:var(--color-slate)]`. Direct WCAG-AA win on
   AiBI-L sessions, MaturityScorecard, course sidebars, and several
   purchase pages.
3. **Resolve Header CTA collision.** Drop the "Assessment" nav link OR
   hide the right-side button on `/`, `/assessment*`. Cleaner visual
   hierarchy on every page in 5 lines.
4. **Distinguish eyebrow conventions.** Either standardize all eyebrows
   to `font-serif-sc tracking-[0.2em]` or split into two roles (serif-sc
   for editorial, mono for system/numbered) and ship as utilities.
5. **Add a "Why now" sourced-stats band on `/`.** Three named-citation
   stats from CLAUDE.md (e.g., FDIC efficiency ratio, Bank Director 66%,
   Gartner 57% skill gap) under a tight `font-mono tabular-nums`
   treatment. Raises institutional authority without buzzword cost.
