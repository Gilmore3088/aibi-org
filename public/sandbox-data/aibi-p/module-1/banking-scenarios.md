# Module 1: Regulatory Landscape — AI Deployment Scenarios

Ten realistic scenarios for a $500M community bank evaluating AI deployment decisions. Each scenario requires identifying applicable regulations, compliance requirements, and examiner focus areas.

---

## Scenario 1: Customer-Facing Chatbot for Account Balance Inquiries

Heritage Community Bank ($510M assets) wants to deploy an AI-powered chatbot on its online banking platform to handle routine account balance inquiries, transaction history lookups, and branch hours questions. The chatbot would authenticate users via their existing online banking session and display real-time account data pulled from the core banking system.

## Scenario 2: Automated Lending Pre-Qualification Decisions

The bank's consumer lending team proposes using an AI model to automate pre-qualification decisions for personal loans under $25,000. The model would analyze applicant income, existing debt, credit bureau data, and deposit account history to generate an instant pre-qualification offer or denial displayed on the bank's website.

## Scenario 3: BSA/AML Transaction Monitoring with ML Models

Heritage's BSA officer wants to replace the bank's rules-based transaction monitoring system with a machine learning model from a regtech vendor. The model would analyze transaction patterns across all deposit accounts to flag suspicious activity, reducing the current 92% false-positive rate while maintaining detection sensitivity for structuring, layering, and unusual wire activity.

## Scenario 4: Using ChatGPT to Draft Board Meeting Minutes

The executive assistant to the CEO has been using a personal ChatGPT account to upload board meeting audio recordings and generate draft minutes. The recordings include discussions of the bank's strategic plan, potential acquisition targets, CAMELS ratings from the most recent exam, and individual borrower names mentioned during credit committee reports.

## Scenario 5: AI-Powered Email Triage for Member Complaint Routing

The bank's operations team wants to deploy an AI email classifier that reads incoming messages to the general customer service inbox and routes them by category: account disputes, fee complaints, fraud reports, loan inquiries, and general questions. The system would read the full email body, extract customer names and account references, and assign each message to the appropriate department queue.

## Scenario 6: Using Claude to Analyze CRA Lending Patterns

A compliance analyst wants to use Claude (via the API) to analyze three years of HMDA and small business lending data to identify potential CRA assessment area gaps. The analyst would upload CSV exports containing loan amounts, census tract codes, borrower income brackets, and approval or denial outcomes to generate a narrative summary of lending patterns by geography and income level.

## Scenario 7: Deploying AI for Automated Appraisal Review

Heritage's real estate lending department is evaluating an AI tool from a fintech vendor that would automatically review residential appraisal reports for completeness, comparable selection quality, and valuation reasonableness. The tool would flag appraisals that deviate more than 10% from its automated valuation model and recommend accept, revise, or reject decisions to the review appraiser.

## Scenario 8: Using Copilot to Generate Compliance Exam Responses

The chief compliance officer wants the compliance team to use GitHub Copilot (integrated into their document editor) to draft responses to regulatory exam findings. Staff would paste examiner comments and supporting documentation into the editor and use Copilot to generate first-draft response letters that address each finding with corrective action plans and timeline commitments.

## Scenario 9: AI-Driven Credit Scoring Model from a Fintech Vendor

A fintech partner proposes that Heritage integrate its proprietary AI credit scoring model as a secondary decisioning layer for small business loans. The model uses alternative data sources including business utility payment history, online review sentiment, and supply chain transaction velocity in addition to traditional bureau data. The vendor describes the model as a "black box" neural network.

## Scenario 10: Using NotebookLM to Research Regulatory Updates

A junior compliance analyst uses Google NotebookLM to upload recent OCC bulletins, FDIC Financial Institution Letters, and Federal Reserve supervision guidance documents. The analyst asks NotebookLM to summarize key changes, identify action items for community banks under $1B in assets, and flag any new requirements that would affect Heritage's existing policies and procedures.
