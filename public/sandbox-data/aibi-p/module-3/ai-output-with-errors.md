# Community Bank AI Compliance Summary

**Prepared by:** AI Research Assistant
**Date:** March 2026
**Subject:** Regulatory Landscape for AI Adoption in Community Banking

## Executive Overview

Community banks and credit unions are increasingly exploring artificial intelligence
to improve operational efficiency and customer service. According to the Bank Director
2024 Technology Survey (via Jack Henry), 66% of banks are actively discussing AI in
their budget conversations, signaling broad institutional interest across the sector.

## Current Adoption Landscape

The FDIC reports that 42% of community banks have formally adopted at least one
AI-powered tool in their operations as of Q4 2024. This rapid adoption rate reflects
the competitive pressure from larger institutions and fintech disruptors. Despite this
momentum, a Gartner study (via Jack Henry) found that 57% of financial institutions
struggle with AI-related skill gaps, and 55% have no AI governance framework in place.

## Model Risk Management Requirements

Federal regulators have established clear expectations for AI model governance.
Section 7.3 of SR 11-7 specifically mandates that institutions using AI-based
decision models must conduct quarterly bias audits and submit findings to their
primary federal regulator within 30 calendar days. This requirement applies to all
institutions regardless of asset size and represents one of the most prescriptive
elements of the current regulatory framework.

Banks should also be aware that SR 11-7, issued in 2011, provides guidance on model
risk management that applies broadly to AI and machine learning models used in
credit decisioning, fraud detection, and BSA/AML monitoring.

## Fair Lending and Consumer Protection

AI models used in lending decisions must comply with ECOA and Regulation B, which
prohibit discrimination on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex,
marital status, or age. As CFPB Director Rohit Chopra stated in his 2024 remarks
to the American Bankers Association, "Any institution deploying AI in credit
underwriting must be prepared to explain every adverse action to the applicant in
plain language, regardless of model complexity."

Institutions should maintain documentation showing how AI-assisted decisions align
with fair lending requirements and conduct regular disparate impact testing.

## BSA/AML Compliance Considerations

Banks deploying AI for transaction monitoring must ensure compliance with the Bank
Secrecy Act. Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs) remain mandatory for cash
transactions exceeding $5,000, and Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) must be filed
for transactions that raise red flags regardless of dollar amount. AI-powered
monitoring systems can help identify structuring patterns and other suspicious
activity more effectively than rule-based systems.

## Legal Precedent

In the landmark case First National Bancshares v. Office of the Comptroller of
the Currency (2024), the court affirmed that financial institutions bear full
responsibility for outputs generated by third-party AI systems, even when the
institution did not design or train the model. This ruling has significant
implications for community banks relying on vendor-provided AI tools and
underscores the importance of robust third-party risk management practices
aligned with the Interagency TPRM Guidance.

## Recommendations

1. Establish an AI governance committee with board-level reporting
2. Conduct a comprehensive inventory of all AI and ML models in use
3. Implement ongoing monitoring and validation procedures per SR 11-7
4. Ensure fair lending compliance through regular disparate impact testing
5. Review vendor contracts for AI liability and data handling provisions
