# Safe AI Use Pledge — Template

The signed statement that closes your AiBI-P credential. Personal. Specific. Dated. Named.

Generic pledges defeat the purpose. Examples below show what specific looks like.

---

## Structure

```
I, [Your Full Name], commit to the following while using AI in my role at [Bank Name]:

[Paragraph 1 — What you WILL use AI for]
Name the actual workflows. Name the human reviewer for each. Name the tools.

[Paragraph 2 — What you WILL NOT do]
List the specific things you commit to not doing — what you will not paste, what
decisions you will not delegate to AI, what AI outputs you will not let leave
the bank without review.

[Paragraph 3 — Tool boundaries]
Name the bank's approved AI tools. Name what tools you will not use for bank work.

[Paragraph 4 — Documentation]
What you will save in the bank's prompt library. How often you will re-review.

[Paragraph 5 — Personal accountability]
Your understanding that catching AI failures is your job, not the AI's. Your
commitment to report failures and your understanding of escalation paths.

Signed: [Your Full Name]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Institution: [Bank Name]
```

---

## Specificity examples

### Generic vs. specific — paragraph 1

❌ Generic: "I will use AI responsibly to support my work."
✅ Specific: "I will use AI to draft the prose section of monthly BSA exception reports from sanitized data, with the BSA Officer reviewing every number against the source register before submission. I will use AI to draft procedure documents from verbal descriptions, with peer review before any procedure becomes policy."

---

### Generic vs. specific — paragraph 2

❌ Generic: "I will not use AI for things that could cause harm."
✅ Specific: "I will not paste customer names, account numbers, balances, addresses, SSNs, examination findings, or any institution-confidential information into any public AI tool. I will not use AI to generate credit decisions, fair-lending analyses, regulatory responses, or examination correspondence."

---

### Generic vs. specific — paragraph 3

❌ Generic: "I will use approved tools."
✅ Specific: "I will use only the bank's approved tool list (currently: Claude via Anthropic API, Microsoft Copilot via the bank's M365 tenant). I will not use ChatGPT (no enterprise license), Google Gemini (not on the approved list), or any AI feature embedded in a SaaS tool that has not been through third-party risk review."

---

## What goes wrong if you keep it generic

The pledge becomes a check-the-box exercise. Examiners reading the credential see "responsible AI use" without specifics, and they ask: "responsible by what standard?" Your specific pledge answers that.

The pledge also fails its OWN purpose — to give you something to look at when a colleague asks "can I just paste this customer email into ChatGPT real quick?" A generic pledge gives you no answer. A specific pledge gives you "no, because that's customer PII into a tool that's not on our approved list, and that's exactly what I committed not to do."

---

## Re-signing

The pledge is signed at credential issuance and re-signed annually. Bank policy may require re-signing after any material change to the bank's AI tool inventory.

---

## Filing

The signed pledge is filed in:

- The bank's credential / training records (compliance-readable)
- The learner's personal copy (kept private)
- Optional: a quote of the WILL/WILL NOT language in the bank's AI Acceptable Use Policy citing aggregate practitioner pledges as evidence of internal commitment

The pledge is not posted publicly. It is an institutional artifact, not a marketing claim.
