# AI Governance Best Practices for Community Banks

**Prepared by:** AI Research Assistant
**Date:** March 2026
**Subject:** Building an Effective AI Governance Framework

## The Case for AI Governance

Community banks face mounting pressure to adopt AI while maintaining regulatory
compliance and institutional trust. According to the Bank Director 2024 Technology
Survey (via Jack Henry), 66% of banks are actively discussing AI budget allocation.
Yet a Gartner study (via Jack Henry) found that 55% of financial institutions have
no AI governance framework in place, and 48% lack clarity on AI business impacts.

## Regulatory Alignment

While no comprehensive AI-specific banking regulation exists yet (per GAO-25-107197,
May 2025), existing supervisory frameworks apply. SR 11-7 (2011) establishes model
risk management expectations. The Interagency TPRM Guidance governs third-party AI
vendor relationships. ECOA and Regulation B apply to any AI used in credit
decisioning. The AIEOG AI Lexicon (February 2026) provides official definitions for
terms including hallucination, AI governance, and human-in-the-loop.

## Workforce Readiness

The skill gap is real: 57% of financial institutions report struggling with
AI-related skill gaps (Gartner, via Jack Henry). Community banks should invest in
structured AI literacy programs that build competency progressively, from awareness
through hands-on proficiency.

## Data Classification

Before deploying any AI tool, institutions must classify their data by sensitivity.
Customer PII, account numbers, credit reports, and SARs require the highest
protection tier under GLBA and FCRA. Internal operational data and process
documentation carry lower risk but still require governance. The principle of
least privilege should guide all AI system access to institutional data.

## Vendor Due Diligence

Community banks increasingly rely on third-party AI vendors. Robust due diligence
should include reviewing data handling practices, model validation procedures,
and contractual liability provisions. PII should never be processed in public
LLM environments without appropriate safeguards and contractual protections.
