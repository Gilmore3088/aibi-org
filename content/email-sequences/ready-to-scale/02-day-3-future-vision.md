The five conditions of a working AI program — daily use, reusable patterns, named review steps, data discipline, measured outcomes — you have most of them. That is what your tier means.

The next question is what compounds. Three behaviors separate institutions that maintain a lead from those that do not:

1. **Codification.** Patterns that work get written into a playbook. The playbook is what survives staff turnover, what onboards the next cohort, and what your examiner reads instead of asking you 40 questions.
2. **Replication.** Every new hire — and every existing employee in a department that has not yet adopted — gets trained on the codified patterns, not the originals. This is what turns one team's win into the institution's baseline.
3. **Forward training.** As the next wave of AI capability arrives — agents that act, multi-step reasoning, model-specific governance — your team is the one that does not have to catch up. It is the institution that already has a culture of structured AI use.

Most peer institutions are still in Building Momentum. Your job is to widen the gap. The mistake at your tier is to treat the early wins as permanent. They are not. The advantage is in the operating discipline that keeps producing them.
