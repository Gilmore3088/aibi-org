The question for institutions at your level is not "how do we adopt." It is "who is the AI judgment in the room when this gets harder."

That is what Leadership Advisory is. The shape:

- A standing role at your leadership cadence — typically monthly, sometimes more.
- Strategic decisions on AI use that require an outside perspective — vendor selection, governance updates, regulator conversations, M&A integration of an institution at a different maturity level.
- Drafting and reviewing examiner-facing artifacts before they go out the door.
- Briefing your board when AI lands on the agenda.

What it is not:

- Not a course.
- Not implementation work.
- Not a deck-and-Q&A engagement.

It is a relationship. Pricing and scope are negotiated for fit, not listed on a page. The conversation is the next step.
