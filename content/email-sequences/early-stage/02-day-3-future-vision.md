A coordinated AI program — the thing your tier is reaching for — looks like this in practice:

1. **Staff use AI for internal workflows daily.** This part you have, in pockets. The work is making it consistent.
2. **Prompts follow consistent, reusable patterns.** Today, three people are using three different approaches to the same task. A program means one approach, documented, repeatable.
3. **Outputs are reviewed before use.** A named review step in the workflow, not "I check it sometimes."
4. **Sensitive data is never exposed.** A documented use policy with approved tools and approved data classes — not folklore.
5. **At least 1–3 workflows produce measurable time savings.** Reported. With numbers. Monthly.

The honest assessment of Early Stage: you are roughly half of the way to all five. The mistake we see is institutions trying to add new use cases before consolidating the ones already working. The faster move is to take one or two existing wins, document them properly, and publish a use policy that codifies what is allowed.

That is exactly what AiBI-Foundation teaches. Twelve modules. Staff capability + governance posture in the same workflow. Your existing experimenters become the first cohort — they do not start from scratch.
