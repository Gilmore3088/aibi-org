# Module 7: Live Skill Workshop -- Skill-Building Scenarios

Five banking tasks a learner can choose to build a reusable AI skill for.
Each scenario includes a department context and a concrete deliverable so the
learner knows exactly what "done" looks like.

---

## 1. Monthly Exception Report Summarization (Operations)

Summarize 200+ exception items into a 1-page report for the ops manager.

**Context:** Every month the operations team exports a spreadsheet of
transaction exceptions -- overdrafts, holds, return items, encoding errors,
and BSA alerts. The ops manager needs a concise summary that highlights
trends, top offenders by category, and items requiring escalation.

**Deliverable:** A single-page narrative with a category breakdown table,
month-over-month trend comparison, and a prioritized action list.

---

## 2. Loan Application Pre-Screening Narrative (Lending)

Generate a preliminary analysis narrative from a loan application package.

**Context:** Before a loan officer conducts a full underwriting review, the
lending team needs a structured first-pass narrative that captures key
applicant data, flags obvious risk factors, and identifies missing
documentation. This narrative accompanies the file when it moves to the
credit analyst.

**Deliverable:** A structured narrative covering applicant summary, income
and debt ratios, collateral overview, preliminary risk flags, and a
documentation checklist with missing-item callouts.

---

## 3. Regulatory Change Impact Assessment (Compliance)

Analyze a new regulatory guidance document and map impacts to current
policies.

**Context:** When a federal or state regulator issues new guidance -- an
interagency statement, an OCC bulletin, or a proposed rule change -- the
compliance team must assess which internal policies, procedures, and controls
are affected and draft a remediation timeline.

**Deliverable:** An impact matrix mapping each guidance requirement to
affected internal policies, a gap analysis identifying where current
practices fall short, and a recommended remediation timeline with owner
assignments.

---

## 4. Board Financial Narrative from Excel Data (Finance)

Convert raw financial data into a narrative summary for the quarterly board
package.

**Context:** The CFO's team prepares a quarterly board package that includes
financial performance commentary. The raw data lives in Excel -- balance
sheet, income statement, and key ratios -- but the board expects a plain-
language narrative that explains what the numbers mean, what changed from
last quarter, and what to watch going forward.

**Deliverable:** A two-page narrative covering earnings performance, net
interest margin trends, asset quality, liquidity position, and capital
adequacy, with quarter-over-quarter and year-over-year comparisons in plain
language suitable for non-financial board members.

---

## 5. Member Communication Template for Rate Changes (Retail)

Draft member-facing communications about deposit rate adjustments.

**Context:** When the ALCO committee approves deposit rate changes, the
retail team needs to notify members promptly and clearly. Communications
must be accurate, comply with Reg DD (Truth in Savings) disclosure
requirements, and maintain a tone that reinforces the institution's
relationship with its members.

**Deliverable:** A multi-channel communication set -- email, in-app
notification, and statement insert -- each tailored to its format, with
Reg DD-compliant disclosures, effective dates, and a comparison showing
old vs. new rates.
